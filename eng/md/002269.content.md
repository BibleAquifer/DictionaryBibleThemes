## 8488: tithing (id: 8488)

The practice of offering to God a tenth of the harvest of the land and of livestock, as holy to the LORD. The idea is also used in a more general sense, meaning offering one\-tenth of one's income to the Lord.

Tithing was practised before the giving of the OT law
-----------------------------------------------------

**[GEN 14:18–14:20](https://ref.ly/Gen14:18-Gen14:20)** *Abraham offered a tenth of the goods taken in battle, apparently in thankfulness to God for victory.* *See also* [GEN 28:22](https://ref.ly/Gen28:22); [HEB 7:1–7:3](https://ref.ly/Heb7:1-Heb7:3)

Tithing under the law
---------------------

### Tithing extends to all kinds of produce and livestock

**[LEV 27:30–27:32](https://ref.ly/Lev27:30-Lev27:32)** *Cereal and fruit offerings could be commuted to money.* *See also* [2CH 31:5–31:6](https://ref.ly/2Chr31:5-2Chr31:6); [NEH 10:35–10:38](https://ref.ly/Neh10:35-Neh10:38), [NEH 13:12](https://ref.ly/Neh13:12)

### All tithes were paid to the Levites

[NUM 18:21–18:24](https://ref.ly/Num18:21-Num18:24); ; [NEH 10:37–10:38](https://ref.ly/Neh10:37-Neh10:38); ; [HEB 7:5](https://ref.ly/Heb7:5)

### Tithes were to be paid in a designated place

[DEU 12:5–12:6](https://ref.ly/Deut12:5-Deut12:6), [DEU 14:22–14:29](https://ref.ly/Deut14:22-Deut14:29), [DEU 26:2](https://ref.ly/Deut26:2) *Tithes were paid in Jerusalem, or every three years in towns where the tithe feast was eaten.*
