## 5616: weights and measures, dry (id: 5616)

These terms derive originally from the containers used, which held a fixed amount. Measures are given in ascending order, with their approximate imperial and metric equivalents.

Cab (1/18 ephah): about 2 pints (about 1 litre)
-----------------------------------------------

**[2KI 6:25](https://ref.ly/2Kgs6:25)** *This is the only mention in Scripture of this measure.*

Omer (1/10 ephah): about 4 pints (about 2 litres)
-------------------------------------------------

**[EXO 16:16–16:22](https://ref.ly/Exod16:16-Exod16:22)** *This incident records the only mention in Scripture of this measure.* *See also* [EXO 16:32](https://ref.ly/Exod16:32), [EXO 16:36](https://ref.ly/Exod16:36)

Seah (1/3 ephah): about 13 pints (about 7\.3 litres)
----------------------------------------------------

**[GEN 18:6](https://ref.ly/Gen18:6)** *The seah was a measure used for flour and cereals.* *See also* [1SA 25:18](https://ref.ly/1Sam25:18); [1KI 18:32](https://ref.ly/1Kgs18:32); [2KI 7:1](https://ref.ly/2Kgs7:1)

Ephah: about 3/5 bushel (about 22 litres)
-----------------------------------------

**[RUT 2:17](https://ref.ly/Ruth2:17)** *The ephah was used only for flour and cereals, and had subdivisions of 1/6 and 1/10\.* *See also* [LEV 5:11](https://ref.ly/Lev5:11), [LEV 24:5](https://ref.ly/Lev24:5); [NUM 15:4–15:9](https://ref.ly/Num15:4-Num15:9); [JDG 6:19](https://ref.ly/Judg6:19); [1SA 17:17](https://ref.ly/1Sam17:17); [EZK 45:10–45:11](https://ref.ly/Ezek45:10-Ezek45:11); [ZEC 5:6–5:8](https://ref.ly/Zech5:6-Zech5:8) *The word for “measuring basket” in the Hebrew is “ephah”.*

Lethek (5 ephahs or 1/2 homer): about 3 bushels (about 110 litres)
------------------------------------------------------------------

**[HOS 3:2](https://ref.ly/Hos3:2)** *Possibly a Phoenician measure, this is the only mention in Scripture of this measure.*

Cor (10 ephahs): about 6 bushels (about 220 litres)
---------------------------------------------------

**[1KI 4:22](https://ref.ly/1Kgs4:22)** *The cor was used for measuring flour and cereals, and also appears to have been used for measuring oil.* *See also* [1KI 5:11](https://ref.ly/1Kgs5:11); [2CH 2:10](https://ref.ly/2Chr2:10), [2CH 27:5](https://ref.ly/2Chr27:5); [EZR 7:22](https://ref.ly/Ezra7:22); [EZK 45:14](https://ref.ly/Ezek45:14)

Homer (10 ephahs): about 6 bushels (about 220 litres)
-----------------------------------------------------

**[LEV 27:16](https://ref.ly/Lev27:16)** *The homer is an older word than the cor, but is equivalent to it. It originally meant “a donkey load” and was widely used throughout the ancient Near East from the second millennium B.C. as a measure for cereals.* *See also* [NUM 11:32](https://ref.ly/Num11:32); [ISA 5:10](https://ref.ly/Isa5:10); [EZK 45:11](https://ref.ly/Ezek45:11), [EZK 45:13–45:14](https://ref.ly/Ezek45:13-Ezek45:14); [HOS 3:2](https://ref.ly/Hos3:2)

Other dry measures found in the NT
----------------------------------

### Quart (choinix): about 1 1/2–2 pints (about 1 litre)

[REV 6:6](https://ref.ly/Rev6:6) *The word translated “quart” is “choinix”, a Greek measure.*

### Saton (equivalent to the OT seah)

[MAT 13:33](https://ref.ly/Matt13:33) parallel [LUK 13:21](https://ref.ly/Luke13:21) *The “large amount” referred to is “three sata” in the Greek.*

### Bowl (modius): about 15 1/2 pints (about 8\.75 litres)

[MAT 5:15](https://ref.ly/Matt5:15) parallels [MRK 4:21](https://ref.ly/Mark4:21), [LUK 11:33](https://ref.ly/Luke11:33) *The word translated “bowl” is the Roman measure “modius”.*

### Koros (equivalent to the OT cor)

[LUK 16:7](https://ref.ly/Luke16:7) *The measure translated as “a thousand bushels” is “one hundred korous” in the Greek.*
