## 7720: elders, in the church (id: 7720)

In the NT a group of leaders responsible for managing and teaching a local church. As the name implies, they were normally men of experience and maturity. Although women played a very significant part in early Christian ministry, there is no specific NT evidence for women as elders.

Elders in the Jerusalem church
------------------------------

**[ACT 15:4](https://ref.ly/Acts15:4)** *See also* [ACT 11:30](https://ref.ly/Acts11:30), [ACT 15:2](https://ref.ly/Acts15:2), [ACT 15:6](https://ref.ly/Acts15:6), [ACT 15:22–15:23](https://ref.ly/Acts15:22-Acts15:23), [ACT 16:4](https://ref.ly/Acts16:4), [ACT 21:18](https://ref.ly/Acts21:18)

Elders are also called \`\`overseers''
--------------------------------------

**[ACT 20:28](https://ref.ly/Acts20:28)** *In [Ac 20:17](https://ref.ly/Acts20:17) they are described as “elders”. Here they are “overseers”.* *See also* [TIT 1:6–1:7](https://ref.ly/Titus1:6-Titus1:7)

Elders are appointed by apostles or their delegates
---------------------------------------------------

**[ACT 14:23](https://ref.ly/Acts14:23)** *Here, as always in the NT, people are appointed to a joint eldership. There is no NT example of a single elder leading a church.* *See also* [TIT 1:5](https://ref.ly/Titus1:5)

Qualifications for elders
-------------------------

**[TIT 1:6–1:9](https://ref.ly/Titus1:6-Titus1:9)** *As in [Ac 20:17,28](https://ref.ly/Acts20:17,Acts20:28), “elder” is here synonymous with “overseer”.* *See also* [1TI 3:1–3:7](https://ref.ly/1Tim3:1-1Tim3:7)
