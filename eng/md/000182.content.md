## 2306: Jesus Christ, as high priest (id: 2306)

Jesus Christ, being a truly human high priest, perfectly represents humanity before God. He made atonement for sins by his own sacrificial death. Being a truly divine high priest, this act of Christ's was perfect, once for all and of eternal value.

The ministry of Jesus Christ as high priest
-------------------------------------------

### He made atonement for sin

**[HEB 2:17](https://ref.ly/Heb2:17)** *See also* [HEB 8:3](https://ref.ly/Heb8:3), [HEB 9:7](https://ref.ly/Heb9:7), [HEB 9:11–9:12](https://ref.ly/Heb9:11-Heb9:12), [HEB 13:11–13:13](https://ref.ly/Heb13:11-Heb13:13)

### He represents human beings before God

**[HEB 5:1](https://ref.ly/Heb5:1)** *See also* [ROM 8:34](https://ref.ly/Rom8:34); [1JN 2:1–2:2](https://ref.ly/1John2:1-1John2:2)

### He entered the Most Holy Place

**[HEB 9:24](https://ref.ly/Heb9:24)** *On the Day of Atonement the high priest entered the Most Holy Place (the sanctuary) which represented the presence of God. Jesus Christ fulfilled this ritual by entering heaven.* *See also* [HEB 4:14](https://ref.ly/Heb4:14), [HEB 6:19–6:20](https://ref.ly/Heb6:19-Heb6:20), [HEB 9:7](https://ref.ly/Heb9:7), [HEB 9:12](https://ref.ly/Heb9:12)

### He makes believers perfect

**[HEB 10:14](https://ref.ly/Heb10:14)** *See also* [HEB 10:10](https://ref.ly/Heb10:10), [HEB 13:12](https://ref.ly/Heb13:12)

### He brings believers close to God

**[HEB 10:19–10:22](https://ref.ly/Heb10:19-Heb10:22)**

### He helps those being tempted

**[HEB 2:18](https://ref.ly/Heb2:18)** *See also* [HEB 4:15](https://ref.ly/Heb4:15), [HEB 5:2](https://ref.ly/Heb5:2)

### He intercedes continually

**[HEB 7:24–7:25](https://ref.ly/Heb7:24-Heb7:25)** *See also* [JHN 17:20](https://ref.ly/John17:20); [ROM 8:34](https://ref.ly/Rom8:34)

Jesus Christ, the human high priest
-----------------------------------

### As a man he is able to represent human beings

**[HEB 2:17](https://ref.ly/Heb2:17)** *See also* [HEB 5:1](https://ref.ly/Heb5:1)

### He is divinely appointed

**[HEB 5:5–5:6](https://ref.ly/Heb5:5-Heb5:6)** *See also* [HEB 3:1–3:2](https://ref.ly/Heb3:1-Heb3:2)

Jesus Christ, the divine high priest
------------------------------------

### He is sinless

**[HEB 7:26–7:27](https://ref.ly/Heb7:26-Heb7:27)** *Jesus Christ is strongly contrasted with other high priests because he did not have to offer sacrifices for his own sins.*

### He is eternal

**[HEB 6:20](https://ref.ly/Heb6:20)** *See also* [HEB 5:6](https://ref.ly/Heb5:6), [HEB 7:17](https://ref.ly/Heb7:17), [HEB 7:21](https://ref.ly/Heb7:21), [HEB 7:28](https://ref.ly/Heb7:28)

### His atoning work was completed once for all

**[HEB 7:27](https://ref.ly/Heb7:27)** *Other high priests had to repeat their sacrifices.* *See also* [HEB 9:12](https://ref.ly/Heb9:12), [HEB 9:25–9:26](https://ref.ly/Heb9:25-Heb9:26), [HEB 9:28](https://ref.ly/Heb9:28), [HEB 10:10](https://ref.ly/Heb10:10)

### He is exalted

**[HEB 8:1](https://ref.ly/Heb8:1)** *See also* [ZEC 6:13](https://ref.ly/Zech6:13); [HEB 7:26](https://ref.ly/Heb7:26)

Jesus Christ's high priesthood was of the order of Melchizedek
--------------------------------------------------------------

**[HEB 6:20](https://ref.ly/Heb6:20)** *The priesthood of Melchizedek resembles that of Jesus Christ because he too was king and had neither beginning nor end.* *See also* [PSA 110:4](https://ref.ly/Ps110:4); [HEB 5:6](https://ref.ly/Heb5:6), [HEB 5:10](https://ref.ly/Heb5:10), [HEB 7:1–7:4](https://ref.ly/Heb7:1-Heb7:4), [HEB 7:14–7:17](https://ref.ly/Heb7:14-Heb7:17)

Responses to Jesus Christ's high priesthood
-------------------------------------------

[HEB 4:14–4:15](https://ref.ly/Heb4:14-Heb4:15) *a call to keep to the faith*; ; [HEB 4:16](https://ref.ly/Heb4:16) *an invitation to seek God's help in times of need*; ; [HEB 10:19–10:22](https://ref.ly/Heb10:19-Heb10:22) *an invitation to draw near to God*; ; [HEB 12:1–12:3](https://ref.ly/Heb12:1-Heb12:3) *a call to run the race of faith*; ; [HEB 12:28–12:29](https://ref.ly/Heb12:28-Heb12:29) *a call to offer thanksgiving and worship*
