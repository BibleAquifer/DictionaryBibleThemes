## 4951: month (id: 4951)

A thirty\-day period of the year measured from one new moon to the next. There were twelve months in the year, with a thirteenth inserted periodically to bring the lunar calendar into line with the solar year. Early month names may have been linked with the seasons. After the exile, Babylonian names were adopted.

Months marked by the new moon
-----------------------------

**[NUM 28:11–28:14](https://ref.ly/Num28:11-Num28:14)** *Special sacrifices marked the first day of each month.* *See also* [PSA 81:3](https://ref.ly/Ps81:3); [ISA 66:23](https://ref.ly/Isa66:23); [EZK 46:6](https://ref.ly/Ezek46:6); [AMO 8:5](https://ref.ly/Amos8:5) *No work was done on the first day of the month.*

Months as periods of 30 days
----------------------------

**[DEU 21:13](https://ref.ly/Deut21:13)** *The prescribed period of mourning was 30 days (a month).* *See also* [NUM 20:29](https://ref.ly/Num20:29); [DEU 34:8](https://ref.ly/Deut34:8)

The first month: Abib/Nisan
---------------------------

**[EXO 12:2](https://ref.ly/Exod12:2)** *Abib, meaning “young head of grain” was the Canaanite name for Israel's first month. Modern equivalent: March\-April.* *See also* [EXO 40:2](https://ref.ly/Exod40:2); [NUM 9:1](https://ref.ly/Num9:1)

### Feasts in the month of Abib

**[DEU 16:1](https://ref.ly/Deut16:1)** *Passover celebrated on 14th Abib.* *See also* [EXO 13:4](https://ref.ly/Exod13:4), [EXO 23:15](https://ref.ly/Exod23:15) *the Feast of Unleavened bread (15th\-21st)*; [LEV 23:5–23:6](https://ref.ly/Lev23:5-Lev23:6) parallel [NUM 28:16–28:17](https://ref.ly/Num28:16-Num28:17); [LEV 23:10–23:11](https://ref.ly/Lev23:10-Lev23:11) *The first sheaf of barley was brought to the priest on 16th Abib.*[NEH 2:1](https://ref.ly/Neh2:1); [EST 3:7](https://ref.ly/Esth3:7)

The second month: Ziv
---------------------

**[1KI 6:1](https://ref.ly/1Kgs6:1)** *Ziv was the pre\-exilic name; the post\-exilic name, Iyyar, does not appear in the Bible. Modern equivalent: April\-May.* *See also* [1KI 6:37](https://ref.ly/1Kgs6:37); [NUM 9:11](https://ref.ly/Num9:11) *A late Passover might be held on 14th Ziv*; [2CH 30:13–30:15](https://ref.ly/2Chr30:13-2Chr30:15) *Hezekiah's celebration of the Passover was in the second month.*

The third month: Sivan
----------------------

**[EST 8:9](https://ref.ly/Esth8:9)** *Modern equivalent: May\-June.* *See also* [EXO 19:1](https://ref.ly/Exod19:1); [EZK 31:1](https://ref.ly/Ezek31:1)

### Pentecost celebrated in the month of Sivan

[LEV 23:15–23:16](https://ref.ly/Lev23:15-Lev23:16) *on 6th Sivan*

The fourth month: Tammuz
------------------------

[EZK 1:1](https://ref.ly/Ezek1:1) *modern equivalent: June\-July*; ; [EZK 8:14](https://ref.ly/Ezek8:14) *Tammuz was a fertility goddess; her annual death was mourned in the month named after her.*

The fifth month
---------------

[NUM 33:38](https://ref.ly/Num33:38) *The name of the fifth month, Ab, does not appear in the Bible. Modern equivalent: July\-August*; ; [2KI 25:8](https://ref.ly/2Kgs25:8)

The sixth month: Elul
---------------------

**[NEH 6:15](https://ref.ly/Neh6:15)** *Modern equivalent: August\-September.* *See also* [EZK 8:1](https://ref.ly/Ezek8:1); [HAG 1:1](https://ref.ly/Hag1:1); [LUK 1:26](https://ref.ly/Luke1:26)

The seventh month: Ethanim
--------------------------

**[1KI 8:2](https://ref.ly/1Kgs8:2)** *Ethanim is the pre\-exilic name; its Babylonian equivalent, Tishri, does not appear in the Bible. Modern equivalent: September\-October.*
