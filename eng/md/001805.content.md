## 7378: high priest, in NT (id: 7378)

Under the Roman administration the high priest remained the senior Jewish leader, but his power was limited by Roman supervision.

High priests of the NT
----------------------

[LUK 3:2](https://ref.ly/Luke3:2); ; [ACT 23:2](https://ref.ly/Acts23:2) *Annas was high priest from A.D. 6 until deposed by the Roman official, Gratus, in A.D. 15\. Though replaced by his son Eleazar and, in A.D. 18, by Caiaphas, his son\-in\-law, Annas retained his authority in Jewish eyes. Strictly, there could only be one high priest at a time. Ananias, the high priest of [Ac 23:2](https://ref.ly/Acts23:2), was not related to the family of Annas.*

---------------------------

[JHN 18:10](https://ref.ly/John18:10), [JHN 18:15](https://ref.ly/John18:15) *The courtyard was within a palace*; ; [ACT 6:12](https://ref.ly/Acts6:12), [ACT 7:1](https://ref.ly/Acts7:1) *The high priest was president of the Sanhedrin.*

The high priest's connections with Jesus Christ
-----------------------------------------------

### Prophesying Jesus Christ's death

**[JHN 11:49–11:50](https://ref.ly/John11:49-John11:50)** *Words spoken by Caiaphas in a political sense were seen to be prophetic, pointing forward to the sacrificing role the high priest would play in the death of Jesus Christ.* *See also* [JHN 11:51–11:52](https://ref.ly/John11:51-John11:52)

### Presiding at Jesus Christ's trial

**[JHN 18:19–18:24](https://ref.ly/John18:19-John18:24)** *See also* [MAT 26:57–26:59](https://ref.ly/Matt26:57-Matt26:59) parallel [MRK 14:53–14:55](https://ref.ly/Mark14:53-Mark14:55); [MAT 26:62–26:65](https://ref.ly/Matt26:62-Matt26:65) parallel [MRK 14:61–14:64](https://ref.ly/Mark14:61-Mark14:64); [LUK 22:54](https://ref.ly/Luke22:54); [JHN 18:28](https://ref.ly/John18:28) *Caiaphas, the necessary go\-between, brought Jesus Christ before Pilate.*

------------------------------------------------
