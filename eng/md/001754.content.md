## 7263: theocracy (id: 7263)

The form of government among the early Israelites in which God was their supreme ruler, and his laws their laws.

The covenant at Sinai established the theocracy
-----------------------------------------------

**[EXO 19:5–19:6](https://ref.ly/Exod19:5-Exod19:6)** *See also* [EXO 19:8](https://ref.ly/Exod19:8), [EXO 24:3](https://ref.ly/Exod24:3), [EXO 24:7–24:8](https://ref.ly/Exod24:7-Exod24:8); [DEU 5:27](https://ref.ly/Deut5:27)

Moses and Joshua renewed the theocratic covenant
------------------------------------------------

[DEU 26:16–26:19](https://ref.ly/Deut26:16-Deut26:19), [DEU 29:9–29:15](https://ref.ly/Deut29:9-Deut29:15); ; [JOS 24:24–24:25](https://ref.ly/Josh24:24-Josh24:25)

Under the theocracy God was the Israelites' supreme ruler
---------------------------------------------------------

**[DEU 33:5](https://ref.ly/Deut33:5)** *“Jeshurun” means “the upright one”, i.e., Israel.* *See also* [NUM 23:21](https://ref.ly/Num23:21); [DEU 33:26](https://ref.ly/Deut33:26); [JDG 8:23](https://ref.ly/Judg8:23); [PSA 145:1](https://ref.ly/Ps145:1)
