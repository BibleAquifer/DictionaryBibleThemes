## 5225: barrenness (id: 5225)

A lack of fertility, especially the inability of a woman to conceive.

Barren land
-----------

### Barrenness describes waste and unproductive land

**[JER 2:6](https://ref.ly/Jer2:6)** *See also* [DEU 32:10](https://ref.ly/Deut32:10); [JOL 2:20](https://ref.ly/Joel2:20)

### Barren land as a sign of God's disfavour

**[GEN 3:18](https://ref.ly/Gen3:18)** *Thorns and thistles are often used to designate barren or unproductive land.* *See also* [HOS 10:8](https://ref.ly/Hos10:8); [HEB 6:8](https://ref.ly/Heb6:8)

Fertility, as opposed to barrenness, a sign of God's blessing
-------------------------------------------------------------

**[EXO 23:25–23:26](https://ref.ly/Exod23:25-Exod23:26)** *Consequently, the inability to conceive was regarded as a sign of God's displeasure.* *See also* [DEU 7:13–7:14](https://ref.ly/Deut7:13-Deut7:14), [DEU 11:14–11:15](https://ref.ly/Deut11:14-Deut11:15); [PSA 113:9](https://ref.ly/Ps113:9); [ISA 41:18](https://ref.ly/Isa41:18)ISA [54:1](https://ref.ly/Isa54:1); [GAL 4:27](https://ref.ly/Gal4:27)

The barren woman
----------------

### Her desire for children

**[GEN 30:1–30:2](https://ref.ly/Gen30:1-Gen30:2)** *See also* [PRO 30:16](https://ref.ly/Prov30:16)

### Examples of barren women

[GEN 16:1](https://ref.ly/Gen16:1) *Sarah*; ; [GEN 29:31](https://ref.ly/Gen29:31) *Rachel*; ; [JDG 13:3](https://ref.ly/Judg13:3) *Manoah's wife*; ; [1SA 2:5](https://ref.ly/1Sam2:5) *Hannah*; ; [LUK 1:7](https://ref.ly/Luke1:7) *Elizabeth*; ; [LUK 1:36–1:37](https://ref.ly/Luke1:36-Luke1:37) *The ability to bear a child in these circumstances, especially in old age, was seen as a sign of God's blessing and his miraculous power.*

### Barrenness was sometimes seen as a sign of God's disfavour

[GEN 16:1–16:4](https://ref.ly/Gen16:1-Gen16:4), [GEN 20:17–20:18](https://ref.ly/Gen20:17-Gen20:18), [GEN 29:31–29:34](https://ref.ly/Gen29:31-Gen29:34); ; [1SA 1:1–1:20](https://ref.ly/1Sam1:1-1Sam1:20); ; [ISA 4:1](https://ref.ly/Isa4:1); ; [LUK 1:5–1:25](https://ref.ly/Luke1:5-Luke1:25)

### Childlessness was not always the result of barrenness

**[DEU 28:41](https://ref.ly/Deut28:41)**

### Barrenness might be an advantage in some circumstances

**[LUK 23:29](https://ref.ly/Luke23:29)** *Referring to the disasters about to come upon Jerusalem.*

Metaphorically, barrenness may describe an unproductive or desperate condition
