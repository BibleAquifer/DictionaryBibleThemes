## 5654: betrothal (id: 5654)

The period of engagement preceding marriage; betrothal was a binding contract established between two families and sealed by the exchange of gifts. During this period the couple did not live together; sexual relations with each other at this stage was regarded as equivalent to adultery. Betrothal describes the relationship between God and his people and between Jesus Christ and the church.

Betrothal and the choice of a spouse
------------------------------------

### Wives were often chosen by parents for their sons

**[GEN 21:21](https://ref.ly/Gen21:21)** *It was usual practice for the groom's parents to choose his wife and arrange the wedding.* *See also* [GEN 24:4](https://ref.ly/Gen24:4), [GEN 38:6](https://ref.ly/Gen38:6)

### Suitable husbands were sought by parents for their daughters

**[RUT 3:1–3:4](https://ref.ly/Ruth3:1-Ruth3:4)** *Naomi, in the role of parent, assumes responsibility to find a husband for Ruth.* *See also* [JDG 1:12–1:13](https://ref.ly/Judg1:12-Judg1:13); [1SA 18:17](https://ref.ly/1Sam18:17), [1SA 18:21](https://ref.ly/1Sam18:21)

### Betrothal following the couple's wishes

**[JDG 14:1–14:4](https://ref.ly/Judg14:1-Judg14:4)** *Sometimes the man chose a prospective bride and his parents negotiated the marriage.* *See also* [GEN 34:1–34:4](https://ref.ly/Gen34:1-Gen34:4), [GEN 24:57–24:58](https://ref.ly/Gen24:57-Gen24:58), [GEN 26:34–26:35](https://ref.ly/Gen26:34-Gen26:35) *Esau chose a bride against his parents' wishes.*

Betrothal preceded marriage
---------------------------

[GEN 29:21](https://ref.ly/Gen29:21); ; [DEU 20:7](https://ref.ly/Deut20:7) *A betrothed soldier was exempted from military service until after his marriage*; ; [MAT 1:24](https://ref.ly/Matt1:24); ; [1CO 7:36–7:38](https://ref.ly/1Cor7:36-1Cor7:38)

An exchange of gifts accompanyed betrothal
------------------------------------------

### The bride\-price

**[GEN 34:11–34:12](https://ref.ly/Gen34:11-Gen34:12)** *This gift, which was given by the groom to the bride's family as compensation, sealed the marriage contract.* *See also* [GEN 24:53](https://ref.ly/Gen24:53), [GEN 29:18](https://ref.ly/Gen29:18), [GEN 29:27](https://ref.ly/Gen29:27); [EXO 22:16–22:17](https://ref.ly/Exod22:16-Exod22:17); [DEU 22:28–22:29](https://ref.ly/Deut22:28-Deut22:29); [2SA 3:14](https://ref.ly/2Sam3:14)

### The dowry

**[1KI 9:16](https://ref.ly/1Kgs9:16)** *The dowry was a gift from the bride's father to the bride and/or groom.* [GEN 24:59](https://ref.ly/Gen24:59), [GEN 29:24](https://ref.ly/Gen29:24), [GEN 29:29](https://ref.ly/Gen29:29); [JDG 1:14–1:15](https://ref.ly/Judg1:14-Judg1:15)

Betrothal was treated as marriage
---------------------------------

**[DEU 22:23–22:24](https://ref.ly/Deut22:23-Deut22:24)** *Though there were no sexual relations within the betrothal period, the commitment was regarded almost as seriously as marriage and infidelity of a betrothed partner was treated as adultery.* *See also* [GEN 19:14](https://ref.ly/Gen19:14) *The men betrothed to Lot's daughters are described as his “sons\-in\-law”*; [MAT 1:18–1:20](https://ref.ly/Matt1:18-Matt1:20) *Mary's pregnancy during betrothal left her open to the charge and consequences of unfaithfulness.*
