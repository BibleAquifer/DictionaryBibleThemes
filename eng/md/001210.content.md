## 5663: childbirth (id: 5663)

Seen by Scripture as a token of God's favour, and used figuratively to picture a number of central biblical themes.

Childbirth in the OT
--------------------

### Childbirth as a blessing

**[GEN 1:28](https://ref.ly/Gen1:28)** *See also* [DEU 7:14](https://ref.ly/Deut7:14); [PSA 127:3–127:5](https://ref.ly/Ps127:3-Ps127:5)

### Childlessness a cause of shame in Israel

**[GEN 30:1](https://ref.ly/Gen30:1)** *See also* [1SA 1:6](https://ref.ly/1Sam1:6), [1SA 1:10–1:11](https://ref.ly/1Sam1:10-1Sam1:11); [LUK 1:24–1:25](https://ref.ly/Luke1:24-Luke1:25)

Childbirth and salvation
------------------------

### The birth of a Saviour

**[GEN 3:15](https://ref.ly/Gen3:15)** *Although childbirth is a focus of judgment, through it the human race continues, and the Messiah comes into the world.* *See also* [GEN 12:2–12:3](https://ref.ly/Gen12:2-Gen12:3), [GEN 15:3–15:6](https://ref.ly/Gen15:3-Gen15:6); [ISA 7:14](https://ref.ly/Isa7:14), [ISA 9:6–9:7](https://ref.ly/Isa9:6-Isa9:7); [MAT 1:18–1:25](https://ref.ly/Matt1:18-Matt1:25); [LUK 1:35](https://ref.ly/Luke1:35), [LUK 2:4–2:7](https://ref.ly/Luke2:4-Luke2:7); [GAL 3:16](https://ref.ly/Gal3:16), [GAL 4:4](https://ref.ly/Gal4:4); [REV 12:5](https://ref.ly/Rev12:5)

### Salvation in childbearing

**[1TI 2:15](https://ref.ly/1Tim2:15)** *A Christian mother fulfils her calling in childbearing; some take it to mean she will be kept safe in childbirth. Some, however, take this verse as a reference to [Ge 3:15](https://ref.ly/Gen3:15), and the coming of Jesus Christ, as above.*
