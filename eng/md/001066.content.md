## 5495: revenge, and retaliation (id: 5495)

Scripture teaches that it is wrong to strike back or to return evil for evil, whether by immediate retaliation or by taking revenge later. Justice must be done, but this must be left in the hands of God, or of the authorities ordained by God.

OT teaching about revenge
-------------------------

### Revenge forbidden in the OT

**[GEN 4:15](https://ref.ly/Gen4:15)**; **[LEV 19:18](https://ref.ly/Lev19:18)**; **[DEU 32:35](https://ref.ly/Deut32:35)**; **[PRO 20:22](https://ref.ly/Prov20:22)** *See also* [DEU 19:5–19:7](https://ref.ly/Deut19:5-Deut19:7), [DEU 19:11–19:13](https://ref.ly/Deut19:11-Deut19:13), [DEU 23:7](https://ref.ly/Deut23:7); [PRO 24:29](https://ref.ly/Prov24:29); [JER 29:7](https://ref.ly/Jer29:7)

### The \`\`lex talionis'' (law of exacting like for like)

**[EXO 21:23–21:25](https://ref.ly/Exod21:23-Exod21:25)** *This was not a rule for personal revenge but to ensure that judicial punishments were neither unjust nor malicious.* *See also* [LEV 24:17–24:21](https://ref.ly/Lev24:17-Lev24:21); [DEU 19:18–19:21](https://ref.ly/Deut19:18-Deut19:21)
