## 6694: mystery (id: 6694)

Normally, God's plan of salvation which, though once unknown to human beings, is now revealed by God.

God's character and purpose cannot be discerned by human reason
---------------------------------------------------------------

**[JOB 11:7](https://ref.ly/Job11:7)** *See also* [JOB 38:1–42:6](https://ref.ly/Job38:1-Job42:6)

God reveals his plans through dreams, interpreted by his servant
----------------------------------------------------------------

**[DAN 2:28](https://ref.ly/Dan2:28)** *Nebuchadnezzar's dreams require the interpretation that God discloses to Daniel before they can be understood as a revelation of divine purposes for Babylon and the surrounding nations.* *See also* [DAN 2:17–2:49](https://ref.ly/Dan2:17-Dan2:49), [DAN 4:9](https://ref.ly/Dan4:9)

### God's mysteries are revealed to a man inspired by his Spirit

**[DAN 4:18](https://ref.ly/Dan4:18)** *See also* [AMO 3:7](https://ref.ly/Amos3:7)

The mystery of God's kingdom
----------------------------

**[MAT 13:11](https://ref.ly/Matt13:11)** parallels [MRK 4:11](https://ref.ly/Mark4:11), [LUK 8:10](https://ref.ly/Luke8:10) *God's plan to establish his kingdom is revealed only to those chosen to receive it. To those “outside” it is hidden in parables.*

Mystery as God's plan of salvation
----------------------------------

### God's plan, now revealed, has been hidden for long ages

**[EPH 3:9](https://ref.ly/Eph3:9)** *The use of “mystery” in this sense is found particularly in Paul's letters.* *See also* [ROM 16:25](https://ref.ly/Rom16:25); [1CO 2:7](https://ref.ly/1Cor2:7); [COL 1:26](https://ref.ly/Col1:26)

### God's plan is fulfilled in Jesus Christ

**[EPH 1:9](https://ref.ly/Eph1:9)** *See also* [EPH 3:4](https://ref.ly/Eph3:4); [COL 2:2](https://ref.ly/Col2:2); [1TI 3:16](https://ref.ly/1Tim3:16) *The “mystery of godliness” is the revealed secret that produces godliness in people.*

### God's plan through Jesus Christ is for the salvation of Gentiles as well as Jews

**[EPH 3:6](https://ref.ly/Eph3:6)** *See also* [ROM 11:25](https://ref.ly/Rom11:25); [COL 1:27](https://ref.ly/Col1:27)

### Ultimately God's plan is for the transformation of the whole creation

**[EPH 1:9–1:10](https://ref.ly/Eph1:9-Eph1:10)** *See also* [1CO 15:51–15:53](https://ref.ly/1Cor15:51-1Cor15:53); [REV 10:7](https://ref.ly/Rev10:7), [REV 17:5–17:8](https://ref.ly/Rev17:5-Rev17:8)

### God's plan is revealed by his Spirit so that his servants may publicly proclaim it

**[EPH 3:8–3:9](https://ref.ly/Eph3:8-Eph3:9)** *See also* [EPH 3:4–3:6](https://ref.ly/Eph3:4-Eph3:6), [EPH 6:19](https://ref.ly/Eph6:19); [COL 1:25–1:27](https://ref.ly/Col1:25-Col1:27), [COL 4:3](https://ref.ly/Col4:3); [REV 10:7](https://ref.ly/Rev10:7)

### Mystery as other aspects of God's purposes, spiritually discerned

**[2TH 2:7](https://ref.ly/2Thess2:7)** *Paul's reference to “the secret power (literally “mystery”) of lawlessness” suggests that we know some things about evil only as God reveals them.* *See also* [1CO 13:2](https://ref.ly/1Cor13:2), [1CO 14:2](https://ref.ly/1Cor14:2)

### Mystery as an allegorical understanding of a biblical text, revealed to Paul by God

**[EPH 5:31–5:32](https://ref.ly/Eph5:31-Eph5:32)** *Paul sees [Ge 2:24](https://ref.ly/Gen2:24) as symbolising the unity between Jesus Christ and his church; the unity of husband and wife is a human reflection of that relationship.*
