## 7304: anointing (id: 7304)

The application of oil was associated with times of rejoicing and celebration. It also possessed a deeper significance, including that of being singled out by God for special favour or responsibilities.

Anointing as a social custom
----------------------------

### In personal grooming

**[ECC 9:8](https://ref.ly/Eccl9:8)** *The associations here are of joy and well\-being.* *See also* [RUT 3:3](https://ref.ly/Ruth3:3); [PSA 92:10](https://ref.ly/Ps92:10); [ISA 57:9](https://ref.ly/Isa57:9); [AMO 6:6](https://ref.ly/Amos6:6); [MAT 6:17](https://ref.ly/Matt6:17)

### Anointing guests as a mark of honour

**[PSA 23:5](https://ref.ly/Ps23:5)** *See also* [LUK 7:36–7:39](https://ref.ly/Luke7:36-Luke7:39), [LUK 7:44–7:47](https://ref.ly/Luke7:44-Luke7:47); [JHN 12:3](https://ref.ly/John12:3)

### Anointing corpses as a burial preparation

**[MRK 16:1](https://ref.ly/Mark16:1)** *See also* [MAT 26:6–26:12](https://ref.ly/Matt26:6-Matt26:12); [JHN 19:38–19:40](https://ref.ly/John19:38-John19:40)

Anointing religious objects
---------------------------

**[EXO 40:9–40:11](https://ref.ly/Exod40:9-Exod40:11)** *Anointed objects are set apart (consecrated), to be used only in the performance of religious ceremonies.* *See also* [GEN 28:18](https://ref.ly/Gen28:18); [EXO 30:22–30:33](https://ref.ly/Exod30:22-Exod30:33)

Anointing people for office
---------------------------

### Priests

**[EXO 40:12–40:15](https://ref.ly/Exod40:12-Exod40:15)** *See also* [LEV 4:16](https://ref.ly/Lev4:16), [LEV 21:10–21:12](https://ref.ly/Lev21:10-Lev21:12); [1CH 29:22](https://ref.ly/1Chr29:22)

### Kings

**[1KI 1:39](https://ref.ly/1Kgs1:39)** *See also* [1SA 10:1](https://ref.ly/1Sam10:1), [1SA 16:12–16:13](https://ref.ly/1Sam16:12-1Sam16:13); [2SA 5:3](https://ref.ly/2Sam5:3); [2KI 11:12](https://ref.ly/2Kgs11:12)

### A prophet

[1KI 19:15–19:16](https://ref.ly/1Kgs19:15-1Kgs19:16) *the only reference to the anointing of a prophet*

Anointing people for other purposes
-----------------------------------

### For purification

[LEV 14:15–14:18](https://ref.ly/Lev14:15-Lev14:18)

### For healing

**[MRK 6:13](https://ref.ly/Mark6:13)** *It has been suggested that anointing in a healing context may be related to the use of oil for medicinal purposes.* *See also* [JAS 5:14](https://ref.ly/Jas5:14)

The figurative use of anointing
-------------------------------

### Anointing by God

**[1SA 26:9](https://ref.ly/1Sam26:9)** *Israel's king is frequently referred to as “the LORD's anointed”. His physical anointing is seen as symbolising a divine anointing. The word “Messiah” literally means “the anointed one”*; **[ISA 45:1](https://ref.ly/Isa45:1)** *Cyrus, as God's agent for a specific task, is referred to as “his anointed”.* *See also* [2SA 23:1](https://ref.ly/2Sam23:1); [PSA 2:2](https://ref.ly/Ps2:2), [PSA 45:2](https://ref.ly/Ps45:2), [PSA 89:20](https://ref.ly/Ps89:20); [EZK 28:14](https://ref.ly/Ezek28:14)

### God's people in the OT

[1CH 16:22](https://ref.ly/1Chr16:22) parallel [PSA 105:15](https://ref.ly/Ps105:15)

### Christian believers

**[2CO 1:21–1:22](https://ref.ly/2Cor1:21-2Cor1:22)** *See also* [1JN 2:20](https://ref.ly/1John2:20), [1JN 2:27](https://ref.ly/1John2:27) *This anointing is not received in an outward ceremony but by sharing in the Holy Spirit's anointing of Jesus Christ.*

Jesus Christ as God's Anointed One (the Messiah)
------------------------------------------------

**[ACT 4:26–4:27](https://ref.ly/Acts4:26-Acts4:27)** *Jesus Christ's receiving of the Holy Spirit at his baptism was his “anointing” for his Messianic work*; **[ACT 10:38](https://ref.ly/Acts10:38)** *Note the link between anointing and the Holy Spirit.* *See also* [DAN 9:25–9:26](https://ref.ly/Dan9:25-Dan9:26); [LUK 4:18](https://ref.ly/Luke4:18); [ISA 61:1](https://ref.ly/Isa61:1); [HEB 1:9](https://ref.ly/Heb1:9)
