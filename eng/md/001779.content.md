## 7335: circumcision, physical (id: 7335)

In the OT, circumcision is seen as an outward sign of membership of Israel, the people of God.

The significance of circumcision in God's covenant with Abraham
---------------------------------------------------------------

**[GEN 17:10–17:14](https://ref.ly/Gen17:10-Gen17:14)** *See also* [ACT 7:8](https://ref.ly/Acts7:8)

### Circumcision is also for Abraham's descendants

[GEN 17:7](https://ref.ly/Gen17:7)

Circumcision as a sign of national identity
-------------------------------------------

Dinah's brothers and the Shechemites: [GEN 34:8–34:9](https://ref.ly/Gen34:8-Gen34:9), [GEN 34:14–34:17](https://ref.ly/Gen34:14-Gen34:17); [EXO 4:24–4:26](https://ref.ly/Exod4:24-Exod4:26); ; [JOS 5:4–5:8](https://ref.ly/Josh5:4-Josh5:8)

Circumcision is integrated into the Mosaic law
----------------------------------------------

[LEV 12:3](https://ref.ly/Lev12:3); ; [JHN 7:22](https://ref.ly/John7:22) *It was later sometimes forgotten that circumcision predated the Mosaic law.*

Circumcision as an important Passover restriction
-------------------------------------------------

[EXO 12:44](https://ref.ly/Exod12:44), [EXO 12:48–12:49](https://ref.ly/Exod12:48-Exod12:49); ; [JDG 14:3](https://ref.ly/Judg14:3) *Some of Israel's neighbours practised circumcision, but not the Philistines.*

Circumcision was not necessarily a sign of consecration to God
--------------------------------------------------------------
