## 5541: society, negative aspects of (id: 5541)

Often pictured as an immoral city or a world of evil, it is regarded as corrupt and godless, hostile to true faith and therefore destined for judgment.

The city as a symbol of corrupt society
---------------------------------------

**[REV 18:2–18:3](https://ref.ly/Rev18:2-Rev18:3)** *Babylon stands for the corrupt Roman state and also for godless society in general.* *See also* [GEN 11:1–11:9](https://ref.ly/Gen11:1-Gen11:9), [GEN 19:1–19:13](https://ref.ly/Gen19:1-Gen19:13); [EZK 22:1–22:5](https://ref.ly/Ezek22:1-Ezek22:5); [MAT 23:37–23:39](https://ref.ly/Matt23:37-Matt23:39) parallel [LUK 13:34–13:35](https://ref.ly/Luke13:34-Luke13:35); [REV 14:8](https://ref.ly/Rev14:8)

\`\`The world'' as a description of society alienated from, and hostile to, both God and his people
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**[GAL 3:22](https://ref.ly/Gal3:22)** *The Greek word translated “world” often means not the physical world, but the world of human life.* *See also* [JHN 15:18](https://ref.ly/John15:18); [EPH 6:12](https://ref.ly/Eph6:12); [1JN 5:19](https://ref.ly/1John5:19); [REV 12:9](https://ref.ly/Rev12:9)

Fallen society becomes pluralistic
----------------------------------

### Israel was continually threatened by the pluralism of the surrounding societies

**[JOS 23:6–23:8](https://ref.ly/Josh23:6-Josh23:8)** *See also* [EXO 23:32–23:33](https://ref.ly/Exod23:32-Exod23:33); [JOS 24:14–24:23](https://ref.ly/Josh24:14-Josh24:23); [1KI 11:1–11:6](https://ref.ly/1Kgs11:1-1Kgs11:6), [1KI 18:16–18:40](https://ref.ly/1Kgs18:16-1Kgs18:40) *Baal was a name used for a variety of local Canaanite deities*; [EZR 9:1–9:2](https://ref.ly/Ezra9:1-Ezra9:2); [ISA 2:6–2:8](https://ref.ly/Isa2:6-Isa2:8); [DAN 3:1–3:12](https://ref.ly/Dan3:1-Dan3:12)

### Pluralism was also a feature of the societies of the NT

**[ACT 17:16–17:23](https://ref.ly/Acts17:16-Acts17:23)** *See also* [1CO 8:4–8:6](https://ref.ly/1Cor8:4-1Cor8:6); [2TI 4:3–4:4](https://ref.ly/2Tim4:3-2Tim4:4); [1JN 5:21](https://ref.ly/1John5:21)

### Pluralism is to be resisted and faith in one God and Saviour maintained

**[1TI 2:5](https://ref.ly/1Tim2:5)** *See also* [ISA 43:10–43:11](https://ref.ly/Isa43:10-Isa43:11); [JHN 14:6](https://ref.ly/John14:6); [ACT 4:12](https://ref.ly/Acts4:12)

Fallen society becomes secular
------------------------------

### Life in a godless society becomes spiritually futile and morally depraved

**[ROM 1:21](https://ref.ly/Rom1:21)** *See also* [ROM 1:18–1:32](https://ref.ly/Rom1:18-Rom1:32); [2TI 3:1–3:5](https://ref.ly/2Tim3:1-2Tim3:5); [REV 18:1–18:3](https://ref.ly/Rev18:1-Rev18:3)

### Israel ignores warnings about becoming a society which has forgotten God

**[PSA 81:11–81:12](https://ref.ly/Ps81:11-Ps81:12)** *See also* [JER 3:19–3:21](https://ref.ly/Jer3:19-Jer3:21); [HEB 3:7–3:11](https://ref.ly/Heb3:7-Heb3:11)

### Israel became an example of a society which, though outwardly religious, lived without reference to God

**[AMO 5:21–5:24](https://ref.ly/Amos5:21-Amos5:24)** *Amos here summarises, and rejects, current religious practices in Israel in the 8th century B.C.* *See also* [AMO 2:6–2:7](https://ref.ly/Amos2:6-Amos2:7), [AMO 5:4–5:15](https://ref.ly/Amos5:4-Amos5:15) *Bethel and Gilgal were popular national shrines, where religion had taken the place of righteousness and relationship*; [AMO 8:4–8:10](https://ref.ly/Amos8:4-Amos8:10)
