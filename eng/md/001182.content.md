## 5619: weights and measures, distance and area (id: 5619)

Diverse expressions were used for these measurements, their being determined in the most practical way possible, reckoned by known averages.

Expressions of distance
-----------------------

### A bow\-shot

**[GEN 21:15–21:16](https://ref.ly/Gen21:15-Gen21:16)**

### Some distance

**[EXO 33:7](https://ref.ly/Exod33:7)** *A variety of distance often lies behind this expression, as can be seen in the examples that follow.* *See also* [GEN 35:16](https://ref.ly/Gen35:16), [GEN 36:6](https://ref.ly/Gen36:6); [NUM 2:2](https://ref.ly/Num2:2); [1SA 26:13](https://ref.ly/1Sam26:13); [2SA 15:17](https://ref.ly/2Sam15:17); [MAT 8:30](https://ref.ly/Matt8:30)

### A Sabbath day's journey

**[ACT 1:12](https://ref.ly/Acts1:12)** *While this is the only mention of this phrase in Scripture, it was in fact a popular expression, indicating the distance a devout Jew could walk on the Sabbath. It was based on the rabbinical interpretation of [Ex 16:29](https://ref.ly/Exod16:29) in the light of [Nu 35:5](https://ref.ly/Num35:5), and was limited to 2,000 cubits.*

Expressions of area
-------------------

### Area expressed by the yoke

**[1SA 14:14](https://ref.ly/1Sam14:14)** *The area translated “about half an acre” in the Hebrew means “half a yoke”. The yoke was the area of land ploughed by a yoke of oxen in one day, approximately one acre.* *See also* [ISA 5:10](https://ref.ly/Isa5:10) *The Hebrew describes the area as “ten\-yoke”, that is, the land ploughed by ten yoke of oxen in one day.*
