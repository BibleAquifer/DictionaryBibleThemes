## 1245: God of the fathers (id: 1245)

God's particular association with Abraham, Isaac and Jacob, the fathers of the nations of Israel, highlights his faithfulness in fulfilling the promises made to them. He is the God who establishes a covenant with his people for their everlasting blessing.

God's relationship with the fathers
-----------------------------------

### The God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob

**[ACT 7:32](https://ref.ly/Acts7:32)** *See also* [EXO 3:6](https://ref.ly/Exod3:6), [EXO 3:15](https://ref.ly/Exod3:15), [EXO 4:5](https://ref.ly/Exod4:5)

### The God of Jacob

**[PSA 20:1](https://ref.ly/Ps20:1)** *“Jacob” is used here as a synonym for the nation of Israel. This usage is common in the Psalms as a reminder to God's people of his faithfulness to Jacob and the emergent nation of Israel.* *See also* [GEN 32:27–32:28](https://ref.ly/Gen32:27-Gen32:28); [PSA 14:7](https://ref.ly/Ps14:7), [PSA 46:7](https://ref.ly/Ps46:7), [PSA 46:11](https://ref.ly/Ps46:11), [PSA 75:9](https://ref.ly/Ps75:9), [PSA 76:6](https://ref.ly/Ps76:6), [PSA 114:7](https://ref.ly/Ps114:7)

### Such a relationship served as an encouragement to others

Abraham's servant was encouraged to trust God in his mission by the example of Abraham's faith: [GEN 24:12](https://ref.ly/Gen24:12), [GEN 24:27](https://ref.ly/Gen24:27), [GEN 24:42](https://ref.ly/Gen24:42), [GEN 24:48](https://ref.ly/Gen24:48); [GEN 26:24](https://ref.ly/Gen26:24), [GEN 31:42](https://ref.ly/Gen31:42), [GEN 31:53](https://ref.ly/Gen31:53); [GEN 46:1–46:3](https://ref.ly/Gen46:1-Gen46:3) *Jacob learned to fear and trust God through his father Isaac.*
