## 7540: Judaism (id: 7540)

Derived from “Judah” (the southern kingdom of the divided monarchy) the term refers to the religion and culture of the Jews from the time of the exile (586 B.C.) onwards.

The exile and the beginnings of Judaism
---------------------------------------

### The people of Judah become known as Jews

[EZR 4:12](https://ref.ly/Ezra4:12); ; [NEH 4:2](https://ref.ly/Neh4:2); ; [EST 3:6](https://ref.ly/Esth3:6); ; [JER 32:12](https://ref.ly/Jer32:12); ; [MAT 2:2](https://ref.ly/Matt2:2); ; [JHN 2:6](https://ref.ly/John2:6)

### Jewish communities were formed in many places

[JER 44:1](https://ref.ly/Jer44:1); ; [EZR 2:1](https://ref.ly/Ezra2:1) *many stayed in Babylon*; ; [JHN 7:35](https://ref.ly/John7:35); ; [ACT 2:8–2:11](https://ref.ly/Acts2:8-Acts2:11)

### Far from the Jerusalem temple, the exiles had difficulty in knowing how to worship

**[PSA 137:4](https://ref.ly/Ps137:4)** *See also* [HOS 3:4](https://ref.ly/Hos3:4) *This prophecy to Israel also expresses the dilemma of the Jerusalem exiles.*

New forms of religion after the exile, probably beginning in Babylon
--------------------------------------------------------------------

### Emphasis on the Scriptures

[NEH 7:73–8:3](https://ref.ly/Neh7:73-Neh8:3)

### Synagogue worship

[LUK 4:16–4:17](https://ref.ly/Luke4:16-Luke4:17) *Unable to sacrifice in the temple, the exiles would have focused their worship on the Scriptures, a practice which developed into synagogue worship.*

### Carefully observing the law

Ezra went from Babylon to Jerusalem with the express intention of seeing that God's law was observed: [EZR 7:11](https://ref.ly/Ezra7:11), [EZR 7:14](https://ref.ly/Ezra7:14), [EZR 7:25](https://ref.ly/Ezra7:25)

The temple and sacrifice
------------------------

### The returning exiles recommence the sacrifices

[EZR 3:1–3:6](https://ref.ly/Ezra3:1-Ezra3:6)

### The temple rebuilt

[EZR 3:8](https://ref.ly/Ezra3:8), [EZR 6:14–6:15](https://ref.ly/Ezra6:14-Ezra6:15)

### The temple as a focus for pilgrims

[JHN 12:20](https://ref.ly/John12:20); ; [ACT 2:5](https://ref.ly/Acts2:5) *They had probably come for Passover and were staying until Pentecost*; ; [ACT 8:27](https://ref.ly/Acts8:27)

### The temple regarded with great reverence

[MAT 26:59–26:61](https://ref.ly/Matt26:59-Matt26:61) parallel [MRK 14:57–14:58](https://ref.ly/Mark14:57-Mark14:58); ; [ACT 6:13](https://ref.ly/Acts6:13), [ACT 21:27–21:29](https://ref.ly/Acts21:27-Acts21:29)

Studying and obeying the law
----------------------------

### Studying Scripture

[JHN 5:39](https://ref.ly/John5:39)

### Total obedience essential

[MAT 23:23](https://ref.ly/Matt23:23); ; [JAS 2:10](https://ref.ly/Jas2:10)

### Human rules added to God's law

[MAT 23:16–23:18](https://ref.ly/Matt23:16-Matt23:18); ; [MRK 7:1–7:13](https://ref.ly/Mark7:1-Mark7:13) parallel [MAT 15:1–15:9](https://ref.ly/Matt15:1-Matt15:9)

### High regard for the Sabbath and traditional rules associated with it

[MAT 12:1–12:2](https://ref.ly/Matt12:1-Matt12:2) parallels [MRK 2:23–2:24](https://ref.ly/Mark2:23-Mark2:24), [LUK 6:1–6:2](https://ref.ly/Luke6:1-Luke6:2)

Groups within NT Judaism
------------------------

### Pharisees

**[MRK 7:3–7:4](https://ref.ly/Mark7:3-Mark7:4)** *The Pharisees stressed the fulfilment of the law by individuals.* *See also* [LUK 11:38](https://ref.ly/Luke11:38), [LUK 18:10–18:12](https://ref.ly/Luke18:10-Luke18:12); [ACT 26:5](https://ref.ly/Acts26:5)

### Sadducees

**[ACT 5:17](https://ref.ly/Acts5:17)** *A powerful minority group, politically astute whilst theologically conservative, they saw temple worship as the chief purpose of the law.* *See also* [MAT 3:7](https://ref.ly/Matt3:7); [MRK 12:18](https://ref.ly/Mark12:18) parallels [MAT 22:23](https://ref.ly/Matt22:23), [LUK 20:27](https://ref.ly/Luke20:27)

### Teachers of the law (scribes)

They preserved, copied, taught and administered the law: [MAT 17:10](https://ref.ly/Matt17:10); ; [MRK 9:14](https://ref.ly/Mark9:14); ; [LUK 2:46](https://ref.ly/Luke2:46); ; [ACT 4:5](https://ref.ly/Acts4:5)

### Zealots

[LUK 6:15](https://ref.ly/Luke6:15) *They favoured a “holy war” to drive out the Romans.*

### Non\-Jewish converts

[MAT 23:15](https://ref.ly/Matt23:15); ; [ACT 2:11](https://ref.ly/Acts2:11), [ACT 6:5](https://ref.ly/Acts6:5), [ACT 13:43](https://ref.ly/Acts13:43)

Judaism and Christianity
------------------------

### Conflict between Jesus Christ and the Jewish authorities

**[MRK 14:1](https://ref.ly/Mark14:1)** *See also* [JHN 11:45–11:50](https://ref.ly/John11:45-John11:50)

### Conflict between the first Christians and Judaism

[JHN 16:2](https://ref.ly/John16:2); ; [ACT 4:1–4:3](https://ref.ly/Acts4:1-Acts4:3), [ACT 5:17–5:18](https://ref.ly/Acts5:17-Acts5:18); ; [GAL 1:13–1:14](https://ref.ly/Gal1:13-Gal1:14)

### Conflict produced by Jewish influence among the first Christians

**[ACT 15:5](https://ref.ly/Acts15:5)** *See also* [ACT 15:1–15:2](https://ref.ly/Acts15:1-Acts15:2); [GAL 2:11–2:16](https://ref.ly/Gal2:11-Gal2:16) *Judaism gave birth to Christianity. After an initial uneasy co\-existence a total break finally ensued.*
