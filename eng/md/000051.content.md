## 1235: God, the LORD (id: 1235)

The translation of the Hebrew name “Yahweh” which is the personal name of God whose meaning was revealed to Moses. It emphasises that God is the one who is eternal, unique, unchangeable and always actively present with his people. It expresses God's role as Israel's Redeemer and covenant Lord.

The name of God, the LORD
-------------------------

### The LORD is the personal name of God

**[ISA 42:8](https://ref.ly/Isa42:8)** *“The LORD” is the NIV rendering of the Hebrew “Yahweh”.*

### The name's meaning

**[EXO 3:14–3:15](https://ref.ly/Exod3:14-Exod3:15)** *This forms part of God's explanation of who he is. “I AM” (in Hebrew closely resembling “Yahweh”) expresses the ever\-present, unchanging, totally dependable character of God.*

### The name is revealed to Moses

**[EXO 6:2–6:3](https://ref.ly/Exod6:2-Exod6:3)** *The name had been used previously (e.g., [Ge 4:26](https://ref.ly/Gen4:26)) but its meaning was not fully understood until God revealed it to Moses in the context of the exodus from Egypt.*

### The name is glorious and awesome

**[DEU 28:58–28:59](https://ref.ly/Deut28:58-Deut28:59)** *See also* [JOS 7:9](https://ref.ly/Josh7:9); [1SA 12:22](https://ref.ly/1Sam12:22)

### The name is not to be misused

**[EXO 20:7](https://ref.ly/Exod20:7)** parallel [DEU 5:11](https://ref.ly/Deut5:11) *The misuse referred to here is swearing falsely by God's name.* *See also* [LEV 19:12](https://ref.ly/Lev19:12), [LEV 24:11](https://ref.ly/Lev24:11), [LEV 24:15–24:16](https://ref.ly/Lev24:15-Lev24:16) *The Jews came to believe that just uttering the name “Yahweh” was an offence and as a result its exact pronunciation has been lost. What merited the death penalty here was using God's name as a curse.*

God reveals the relevance of his name the LORD by his actions
-------------------------------------------------------------

### In creation

**[JER 33:2](https://ref.ly/Jer33:2)** *See also* [ISA 42:5](https://ref.ly/Isa42:5), [ISA 43:15](https://ref.ly/Isa43:15) *“The LORD” is Creator of the universe but also the Creator of the people Israel*; [ISA 45:18](https://ref.ly/Isa45:18) *Human beings are the purpose of God's creation; they know God as “the LORD”.*

### In the deliverance of his people from Egypt

**[EXO 6:6](https://ref.ly/Exod6:6)** *Deliverance is a central concept in the revelation of the divine name.* *See also* [EXO 12:51](https://ref.ly/Exod12:51), [EXO 14:30](https://ref.ly/Exod14:30), [EXO 16:6](https://ref.ly/Exod16:6), [EXO 20:2](https://ref.ly/Exod20:2); [LEV 26:13](https://ref.ly/Lev26:13); [PSA 81:10](https://ref.ly/Ps81:10)

### In establishing a covenant with his people

**[EXO 6:7](https://ref.ly/Exod6:7)** *See also* [EXO 29:46](https://ref.ly/Exod29:46); [2SA 7:24](https://ref.ly/2Sam7:24) parallel [1CH 17:22](https://ref.ly/1Chr17:22); [JER 14:9](https://ref.ly/Jer14:9)

### In the judgment of his enemies

**[EXO 12:12](https://ref.ly/Exod12:12)** *See also* [EXO 7:5](https://ref.ly/Exod7:5), [EXO 8:22–8:23](https://ref.ly/Exod8:22-Exod8:23); [1SA 17:45–17:47](https://ref.ly/1Sam17:45-1Sam17:47); [2CH 14:11–14:13](https://ref.ly/2Chr14:11-2Chr14:13)

### In the restoration of his people

**[EZK 36:36](https://ref.ly/Ezek36:36)** *See also* [PSA 102:15–102:22](https://ref.ly/Ps102:15-Ps102:22); [ISA 60:15–60:22](https://ref.ly/Isa60:15-Isa60:22); [EZK 37:6](https://ref.ly/Ezek37:6), [EZK 37:13–37:14](https://ref.ly/Ezek37:13-Ezek37:14) *The theme “then you, my people, will know that I am the LORD” (with slight variations) runs throughout the book of Ezekiel and is connected to divine acts of judgment and restoration.*

God, the LORD denotes God's lordship
------------------------------------

### He is the Sovereign LORD

**[EZK 2:4](https://ref.ly/Ezek2:4)** *“Sovereign LORD” is the NIV rendering of “Adonai Yahweh”, the favourite divine title in Ezekiel.* *See also* [GEN 15:2](https://ref.ly/Gen15:2), [GEN 15:8](https://ref.ly/Gen15:8); [DEU 3:24](https://ref.ly/Deut3:24); [JOS 7:7](https://ref.ly/Josh7:7); [JDG 6:22](https://ref.ly/Judg6:22); [2SA 7:18–7:29](https://ref.ly/2Sam7:18-2Sam7:29); [PSA 73:28](https://ref.ly/Ps73:28); [ISA 61:1](https://ref.ly/Isa61:1); [JER 1:6](https://ref.ly/Jer1:6); [AMO 1:8](https://ref.ly/Amos1:8)

### He is the LORD Almighty

**[1SA 1:3](https://ref.ly/1Sam1:3)** *“The LORD Almighty” is the NIV rendering of “Yahweh Sabaoth” (“the LORD of hosts”) which emphasises the sovereignty of “the LORD”.* *See also* [1CH 11:9](https://ref.ly/1Chr11:9); [PSA 24:10](https://ref.ly/Ps24:10), [PSA 46:7](https://ref.ly/Ps46:7), [PSA 46:11](https://ref.ly/Ps46:11); [ISA 2:12](https://ref.ly/Isa2:12); [JER 39:16](https://ref.ly/Jer39:16); [HAG 2:6–2:9](https://ref.ly/Hag2:6-Hag2:9); [ZEC 1:3–1:4](https://ref.ly/Zech1:3-Zech1:4), [ZEC 1:6](https://ref.ly/Zech1:6); [MAL 1:14](https://ref.ly/Mal1:14)

### He is the Lord, the LORD Almighty

**[ISA 1:24](https://ref.ly/Isa1:24)** *“The Lord, the LORD Almighty” is the NIV rendering of “Adonai Yahweh Sabaoth” which is often associated with the LORD's authority to rule and to judge.* *See also* [PSA 69:6](https://ref.ly/Ps69:6); [ISA 3:15](https://ref.ly/Isa3:15), [ISA 19:4](https://ref.ly/Isa19:4); [JER 2:19](https://ref.ly/Jer2:19), [JER 46:10](https://ref.ly/Jer46:10), [JER 49:5](https://ref.ly/Jer49:5); [AMO 9:5](https://ref.ly/Amos9:5)

As the LORD, God interacts with his people
------------------------------------------

### He enforces the moral code

**[LEV 18:1–18:5](https://ref.ly/Lev18:1-Lev18:5)** *The phrase “I am the LORD” occurs over 40 times in Leviticus chapters 18–26 and is the authority behind the moral law given to God's people.* *See also* [LEV 11:44–11:45](https://ref.ly/Lev11:44-Lev11:45), [LEV 19:2](https://ref.ly/Lev19:2)

### He makes his people holy

**[LEV 22:32](https://ref.ly/Lev22:32)** *See also* [EXO 31:13](https://ref.ly/Exod31:13); [LEV 20:8](https://ref.ly/Lev20:8), [LEV 21:8](https://ref.ly/Lev21:8); [EZK 20:12](https://ref.ly/Ezek20:12), [EZK 37:28](https://ref.ly/Ezek37:28)

### He is the object of his people's worship

**[EXO 4:22–4:23](https://ref.ly/Exod4:22-Exod4:23)** *The purpose of Israel's redemption from Egypt was that they would be free to worship the LORD.* *See also* [EXO 12:31](https://ref.ly/Exod12:31), [EXO 23:25](https://ref.ly/Exod23:25); [1CH 16:29](https://ref.ly/1Chr16:29) parallel [PSA 96:9](https://ref.ly/Ps96:9); [PSA 29:2](https://ref.ly/Ps29:2), [PSA 99:5](https://ref.ly/Ps99:5), [PSA 100:2](https://ref.ly/Ps100:2); [JON 1:9](https://ref.ly/Jonah1:9)

Jesus Christ takes God's name to himself
----------------------------------------

**[JHN 8:58–8:59](https://ref.ly/John8:58-John8:59)** *The form of expression used by Jesus Christ meant that he was clearly identifying himself with the divine name “I AM”. The response of the crowd confirms this.*
