## 8747: false gods (id: 8747)

Any gods other than the LORD himself. Some of these divinities took the form of images, others were mythical. Some Israelites became involved in idolatrous worship of such gods. The book of Acts records attempts to deify human beings.

False gods associated with foreign nations in the OT
----------------------------------------------------

### Amon, the chief god of Egypt

*See also* [JER 46:25](https://ref.ly/Jer46:25)

### Asherah, a Canaanite goddess

**[EXO 34:13–34:14](https://ref.ly/Exod34:13-Exod34:14)** *Asherah was the consort of El, the chief Canaanite god. Wooden poles, perhaps carved in her image, were often set up in her honour and placed near other pagan objects of worship.* *See also* [DEU 7:5](https://ref.ly/Deut7:5); [JDG 6:25–6:30](https://ref.ly/Judg6:25-Judg6:30) *Gideon destroys an Asherah pole*; [1KI 14:15](https://ref.ly/1Kgs14:15), [1KI 14:23](https://ref.ly/1Kgs14:23), [1KI 15:13](https://ref.ly/1Kgs15:13), [1KI 16:33](https://ref.ly/1Kgs16:33), [1KI 18:19](https://ref.ly/1Kgs18:19) *Elijah summons 400 prophets of Asherah to Mount Carmel.*[2KI 23:4–23:7](https://ref.ly/2Kgs23:4-2Kgs23:7), [2KI 23:13–23:16](https://ref.ly/2Kgs23:13-2Kgs23:16)[ISA 27:9](https://ref.ly/Isa27:9); [JER 17:2](https://ref.ly/Jer17:2); [MIC 5:14](https://ref.ly/Mic5:14)

### Ashtoreth, a goddess of war and fertility

**[JDG 2:12–2:13](https://ref.ly/Judg2:12-Judg2:13)** *Ashtoreth, the consort of Baal, was associated with the evening star and was worshipped as Ishtar in Babylon and as Athtart in Aram. To the Greeks she was Astarte or Aphrodite and to the Romans, Venus.* *See also* [JDG 10:6](https://ref.ly/Judg10:6); [1SA 7:3–7:4](https://ref.ly/1Sam7:3-1Sam7:4), [1SA 12:10](https://ref.ly/1Sam12:10), [1SA 31:10](https://ref.ly/1Sam31:10); [1KI 11:5](https://ref.ly/1Kgs11:5), [1KI 11:33](https://ref.ly/1Kgs11:33)

### Baal, a Canaanite and Phoenician god of fertility and rain

**[JDG 2:10–2:13](https://ref.ly/Judg2:10-Judg2:13)** *Baal, meaning “lord”, was pictured standing on a bull, a popular symbol of fertility and strength. Baal was associated with Asherah and Ashtoreth, goddesses of fertility.*

### Baal\-Zebub, a popular deity of the Philistines

**[MAT 12:24](https://ref.ly/Matt12:24)** parallels [MRK 3:22](https://ref.ly/Mark3:22), [LUK 11:15](https://ref.ly/Luke11:15) *Beelzebub is the Greek form of the Hebrew name “Baal\-Zebub”, meaning “lord of the flies”.* *See also* [2KI 1:1–1:6](https://ref.ly/2Kgs1:1-2Kgs1:6), [2KI 1:16–1:17](https://ref.ly/2Kgs1:16-2Kgs1:17)

### Bel, the chief deity of Babylon

**[ISA 46:1](https://ref.ly/Isa46:1)** *Bel was another name for the sun god, Marduk. Nebo, the god of learning and writing was the son of Marduk.* *See also* [JER 50:2](https://ref.ly/Jer50:2), [JER 51:44](https://ref.ly/Jer51:44)

**[ACT 19:24–19:28](https://ref.ly/Acts19:24-Acts19:28)** *Artemis was the Greek name for the Roman goddess, Diana.*
