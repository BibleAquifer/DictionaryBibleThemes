## 5275: cremation (id: 5275)

The burning of a dead body. Cremation was not practised by Israel or the first Christians. In difficult circumstances a corpse might be burnt and the remains buried
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Cremation of corpses
--------------------

**[1SA 31:12–31:13](https://ref.ly/1Sam31:12-1Sam31:13)** *This was done to prevent any further abuse of the bodies by the Philistines. David later reburied the remains in a family grave (see [2Sa 21:12–14](https://ref.ly/2Sam21:12-2Sam21:14)).*

Burning a body as a punishment
------------------------------

*See also* [LEV 20:14](https://ref.ly/Lev20:14), [LEV 21:9](https://ref.ly/Lev21:9); [JOS 7:15](https://ref.ly/Josh7:15), [JOS 7:25](https://ref.ly/Josh7:25); [2KI 23:20](https://ref.ly/2Kgs23:20)

Funeral fires for the kings of Judah
------------------------------------

These were not for the purpose of cremating the king's corpse, but public demonstrations in his honour: [2CH 16:14](https://ref.ly/2Chr16:14); ; [JER 34:5](https://ref.ly/Jer34:5)
