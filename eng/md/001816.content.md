## 7400: New Year, the (id: 7400)

With the exception of one reference ([Ex 12:2](https://ref.ly/Exod12:2)) the New Year is never specifically dated. The religious calendar apparently began in the spring and the agricultural calendar in the autumn. Later Judaism began its calendar in the autumn.

Evidence that the autumn was regarded as the New Year
-----------------------------------------------------

### The dedication of Solomon's temple

**[1KI 8:2](https://ref.ly/1Kgs8:2)** *Solomon waited 11 months after the completion of the temple (see [1Ki 6:38](https://ref.ly/1Kgs6:38)), before dedicating it, most likely because the great Feast of Tabernacles marked the beginning of the year.*

### Instructions for celebrating the Year of Jubilee

**[LEV 25:8–25:9](https://ref.ly/Lev25:8-Lev25:9)** *The Year of Jubilee was to be proclaimed on the tenth day of the seventh month, which was also the Day of Atonement.* *See also* [LEV 16:29](https://ref.ly/Lev16:29)

### Ezekiel's vision of the new temple

**[EZK 40:1](https://ref.ly/Ezek40:1)** *This vision of the new temple takes place at the beginning of the year. The “tenth of the month” refers presumably to the seventh month.*

### Jeroboam's institution of an autumnal festival

**[1KI 12:32](https://ref.ly/1Kgs12:32)** *This autumn festival was clearly intended to be equivalent not only to the feast in Judah, but also to other pagan New Year festivals, in spite of the eighth month being distinctive.* *See also* [1KI 12:33](https://ref.ly/1Kgs12:33)

Evidence that the spring was regarded as the New Year
-----------------------------------------------------

### The institution of the Passover

**[EXO 12:2](https://ref.ly/Exod12:2)** *This clearly links the spring Feast of Passover to the New Year.* *See also* [LEV 23:5](https://ref.ly/Lev23:5); [NUM 9:2](https://ref.ly/Num9:2)

### Ezekiel's vision of the worship of the new temple

**[EZK 45:18–45:19](https://ref.ly/Ezek45:18-Ezek45:19)** *This purification of Ezekiel's (ideal) temple resembles the Day of Atonement but takes place in the spring.* *See also* [EZK 45:21](https://ref.ly/Ezek45:21)
