## 9125: footstool (id: 9125)

Used mostly symbolically to refer to God's supreme authority over his creation. It is used especially to refer to his final victory over all those who oppose him.

The footstool as a rest for the feet
------------------------------------

**[2CH 9:18](https://ref.ly/2Chr9:18)**; **[JAS 2:3](https://ref.ly/Jas2:3)**

The footstool used symbolically
-------------------------------

### God's footstool as a symbol for the ark and the temple

**[PSA 132:7](https://ref.ly/Ps132:7)** *See also* [1CH 28:2](https://ref.ly/1Chr28:2); [PSA 99:5](https://ref.ly/Ps99:5); [LAM 2:1](https://ref.ly/Lam2:1)

### God's footstool as a symbol for the earth

**[ISA 66:1](https://ref.ly/Isa66:1)** *God cannot be confined to any man\-made temple.* *See also* [MAT 5:35](https://ref.ly/Matt5:35); [ACT 7:49](https://ref.ly/Acts7:49)

### Defeated enemies can be regarded as a footstool

[JOS 10:24–10:25](https://ref.ly/Josh10:24-Josh10:25); ; [1KI 5:3](https://ref.ly/1Kgs5:3); ; [PSA 66:12](https://ref.ly/Ps66:12); ; [ISA 51:23](https://ref.ly/Isa51:23); ; [MIC 7:10](https://ref.ly/Mic7:10)

### Jesus Christ will make all God's enemies his footstool

**[HEB 1:13](https://ref.ly/Heb1:13)** *This passage affirms Jesus Christ's supremacy over the angels.* *See also* [PSA 110:1](https://ref.ly/Ps110:1); [MAT 22:44](https://ref.ly/Matt22:44) parallels [MRK 12:36](https://ref.ly/Mark12:36), [LUK 20:43](https://ref.ly/Luke20:43); [ACT 2:34–2:35](https://ref.ly/Acts2:34-Acts2:35); [EPH 1:22](https://ref.ly/Eph1:22)

### Jesus Christ will complete his victory when he returns

**[HEB 10:13](https://ref.ly/Heb10:13)** *See also* [1CO 15:25](https://ref.ly/1Cor15:25); [HEB 2:8](https://ref.ly/Heb2:8); [PSA 8:6](https://ref.ly/Ps8:6)
