## 5676: divorce, in OT (id: 5676)

Usually initiated by the husband who issued his wife with a certificate of divorce and sent her from his home, divorce broke the marriage bond and allowed parties to remarry. It was not part of God's original purpose for marriage, and is permitted only because of human sinfulness. God is depicted as taking divorce proceedings against adulterous Israel.

The nature of divorce
---------------------

### Divorce dissolves a marriage

**[MRK 10:4](https://ref.ly/Mark10:4)** parallel [MAT 19:7](https://ref.ly/Matt19:7) *The certificate of divorce was a legal document ending the marriage.* *See also* [LEV 22:12–22:13](https://ref.ly/Lev22:12-Lev22:13) *Divorce ends the restrictions on a priest's daughter which were the result of her marriage*; [NUM 30:9](https://ref.ly/Num30:9) *Following divorce a husband is no longer responsible for his wife*; [DEU 24:1–24:2](https://ref.ly/Deut24:1-Deut24:2) *Divorce properly entered into allows the parties to remarry*; [HOS 2:2](https://ref.ly/Hos2:2); [MAT 5:31](https://ref.ly/Matt5:31)

### Divorce was not God's original intention

**[MAL 2:10–2:16](https://ref.ly/Mal2:10-Mal2:16)** *God's displeasure at those of his people who divorced their Jewish wives to marry foreigners, thus breaking faith with their partners and with God*; **[MAT 19:4–19:8](https://ref.ly/Matt19:4-Matt19:8)** parallel [MRK 10:5–10:9](https://ref.ly/Mark10:5-Mark10:9) *The Law of Moses did not command divorce, but regulated an existing practice, particularly to protect the wife.* *See also* [GEN 1:27](https://ref.ly/Gen1:27), [GEN 2:24](https://ref.ly/Gen2:24)

Circumstances permitting divorce
--------------------------------

### Displeasure

**[DEU 24:1](https://ref.ly/Deut24:1)** *See also* [DEU 21:13–21:14](https://ref.ly/Deut21:13-Deut21:14); [MAT 19:3](https://ref.ly/Matt19:3)

### Returning exiles were required to divorce their foreign wives

**[EZR 10:10–10:11](https://ref.ly/Ezra10:10-Ezra10:11)** *See also* [EZR 10:2–10:3](https://ref.ly/Ezra10:2-Ezra10:3)
