## 4260: rivers and streams (id: 4260)

The term river is used to refer to all kinds of watercourses, including wadis (dried\-up desert river beds) and permanent rivers. It is also used symbolically to refer to things which threaten or overwhelm people and nations, such as invading nations, the peace of God and the Holy Spirit.

Different kinds of rivers and streams
-------------------------------------

### Wadis

**[1KI 17:7](https://ref.ly/1Kgs17:7)** *Wadis were dry river beds in summer but became raging torrents in the rainy season.* *See also* [GEN 32:22](https://ref.ly/Gen32:22) *The Jabbok was a wadi, here in full flow*; [NUM 21:13–21:15](https://ref.ly/Num21:13-Num21:15); [JOB 6:15–6:17](https://ref.ly/Job6:15-Job6:17); [JER 15:18](https://ref.ly/Jer15:18)

### Springs

**[PSA 104:10](https://ref.ly/Ps104:10)** *See also* [GEN 16:7](https://ref.ly/Gen16:7); [EXO 15:27](https://ref.ly/Exod15:27); [JDG 1:14–1:15](https://ref.ly/Judg1:14-Judg1:15); [2KI 2:19–2:22](https://ref.ly/2Kgs2:19-2Kgs2:22)

### Permanent rivers

**[GEN 2:10–2:14](https://ref.ly/Gen2:10-Gen2:14)** *See also* [GEN 41:1–41:3](https://ref.ly/Gen41:1-Gen41:3); [NUM 22:1](https://ref.ly/Num22:1); [DEU 1:7](https://ref.ly/Deut1:7); [ISA 18:1](https://ref.ly/Isa18:1)

### Canals

**[DAN 8:2](https://ref.ly/Dan8:2)** *See also* [EXO 7:19](https://ref.ly/Exod7:19); [EZR 8:21](https://ref.ly/Ezra8:21); [EZK 1:1](https://ref.ly/Ezek1:1) *Although called a river here, the Kebar was a canal of the Euphrates.*

Some significant rivers and streams in Scripture
------------------------------------------------

### River Arnon

The Arnon flowed through Moab to the mid point of the Dead Sea: [NUM 21:13–21:15](https://ref.ly/Num21:13-Num21:15); ; [JDG 11:18](https://ref.ly/Judg11:18); ; [ISA 16:2](https://ref.ly/Isa16:2); ; [JER 48:20](https://ref.ly/Jer48:20)

### Rivers of Damascus

[2KI 5:12](https://ref.ly/2Kgs5:12) *The Abana flowed through the city of Damascus and the Pharpar just to the south of it.*

### River Euphrates

The Euphrates flowed through Mesopotamia for some 1,700 miles (2,700 km) from its source in eastern Turkey to the Persian Gulf: [DEU 1:7](https://ref.ly/Deut1:7); ; [2KI 23:29](https://ref.ly/2Kgs23:29) parallel [2CH 35:20](https://ref.ly/2Chr35:20); ; [ISA 11:15](https://ref.ly/Isa11:15); ; [JER 51:63–51:64](https://ref.ly/Jer51:63-Jer51:64); ; [REV 16:12](https://ref.ly/Rev16:12)

### River Habor

[2KI 17:6](https://ref.ly/2Kgs17:6) parallel [2KI 18:11](https://ref.ly/2Kgs18:11) *The Habor was a tributary of the Euphrates.*

### River Jabbok

The Jabbok flowed westward through Gilead into the Jordan about 20 miles (32 km) north of the Dead Sea: [GEN 32:22](https://ref.ly/Gen32:22); ; [NUM 21:23–21:24](https://ref.ly/Num21:23-Num21:24); ; [DEU 3:16](https://ref.ly/Deut3:16); ; [JDG 11:13](https://ref.ly/Judg11:13)

### River Jordan

The Jordan flowed from the slopes of Mount Hermon through Lake Huleh and the Sea of Galilee, and on into the Dead Sea, providing a fertile basin on either side of the river: [GEN 13:10–13:11](https://ref.ly/Gen13:10-Gen13:11), [GEN 32:9–32:10](https://ref.ly/Gen32:9-Gen32:10); ; [NUM 22:1](https://ref.ly/Num22:1); ; [JOS 3:14–4:9](https://ref.ly/Josh3:14-Josh4:9); ; [2KI 5:8–5:14](https://ref.ly/2Kgs5:8-2Kgs5:14); ; [MRK 1:4–1:5](https://ref.ly/Mark1:4-Mark1:5) parallel [MAT 3:5–3:6](https://ref.ly/Matt3:5-Matt3:6)

### River Kebar

The Kebar was a canal of the Euphrates in Babylon where the exiles gathered to pray: [EZK 1:1](https://ref.ly/Ezek1:1), [EZK 3:15](https://ref.ly/Ezek3:15), [EZK 43:1–43:3](https://ref.ly/Ezek43:1-Ezek43:3)

### River Kerith

[1KI 17:2–17:7](https://ref.ly/1Kgs17:2-1Kgs17:7) *The exact location of this wadi is uncertain, but it ran from the east into the Jordan or one of its tributaries.*

### River Kishon

The Kishon flowed north\-west across the Plain of Megiddo and could quickly flood as a result of storms: [JDG 4:6–4:7](https://ref.ly/Judg4:6-Judg4:7), [JDG 4:13](https://ref.ly/Judg4:13), [JDG 5:21](https://ref.ly/Judg5:21); ; [1KI 18:40](https://ref.ly/1Kgs18:40); ; [PSA 83:9](https://ref.ly/Ps83:9)

### River Nile

The Nile flowed north some 3,500 miles (5,600 km) from Lake Victoria to the Mediterranean, providing a natural irrigation system in the desert through its annual floodings: [EXO 1:22–2:10](https://ref.ly/Exod1:22-Exod2:10), [EXO 7:17–7:21](https://ref.ly/Exod7:17-Exod7:21); ; [ISA 19:5–19:8](https://ref.ly/Isa19:5-Isa19:8); ; [EZK 29:3–29:5](https://ref.ly/Ezek29:3-Ezek29:5)

### River Tigris

The Tigris flowed from the Armenian mountains through Mesopotamia to join the Euphrates some 40 miles (65 km) north of the Persian Gulf: [GEN 2:14](https://ref.ly/Gen2:14); ; [DAN 10:4–10:5](https://ref.ly/Dan10:4-Dan10:5); ; [NAM 2:6–2:8](https://ref.ly/Nah2:6-Nah2:8)
