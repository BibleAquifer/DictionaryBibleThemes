## 5188: tearing of clothes (id: 5188)

Primarily associated with mourning, such action was an expression of deep sorrow and heartfelt grief. It was also a natural reaction at times of great distress and in cases of sincere repentance.

The tearing of clothes was a sign of mourning
---------------------------------------------

### It was a demonstration of grief

**[2SA 1:11–1:12](https://ref.ly/2Sam1:11-2Sam1:12)** *See also* [2SA 13:30–13:31](https://ref.ly/2Sam13:30-2Sam13:31); [2KI 2:11–2:12](https://ref.ly/2Kgs2:11-2Kgs2:12); [JOB 1:20](https://ref.ly/Job1:20), [JOB 2:12](https://ref.ly/Job2:12)

### It was often associated with wearing sackcloth

**[GEN 37:34](https://ref.ly/Gen37:34)** *See also* [2SA 3:31–3:32](https://ref.ly/2Sam3:31-2Sam3:32)

### The high priest was forbidden to tear his clothes in mourning

**[LEV 21:10–21:12](https://ref.ly/Lev21:10-Lev21:12)** *See also* [LEV 10:1–10:7](https://ref.ly/Lev10:1-Lev10:7)

The tearing of clothes indicated deep distress
----------------------------------------------

**[JDG 11:34–11:35](https://ref.ly/Judg11:34-Judg11:35)** *Jephthah had vowed to sacrifice whatever came out of the door of his house to meet him.* *See also* [GEN 37:29](https://ref.ly/Gen37:29), [GEN 44:12–44:13](https://ref.ly/Gen44:12-Gen44:13); [NUM 14:1–14:9](https://ref.ly/Num14:1-Num14:9); [JOS 7:3–7:6](https://ref.ly/Josh7:3-Josh7:6); [2KI 6:26–6:30](https://ref.ly/2Kgs6:26-2Kgs6:30), [2KI 18:28–19:4](https://ref.ly/2Kgs18:28-2Kgs19:4) parallel [ISA 36:13–37:4](https://ref.ly/Isa36:13-Isa37:4); [EST 3:13–4:1](https://ref.ly/Esth3:13-Esth4:1); [ACT 14:8–14:18](https://ref.ly/Acts14:8-Acts14:18)

Repentance was sometimes accompanied by the tearing of clothes
--------------------------------------------------------------

**[1KI 21:20–21:27](https://ref.ly/1Kgs21:20-1Kgs21:27)** *See also* [JER 36:21–36:24](https://ref.ly/Jer36:21-Jer36:24) *an indication of the lack of repentance*; [JOL 2:12–2:14](https://ref.ly/Joel2:12-Joel2:14) *God requires that the tearing of clothes should express genuine repentance.*
