## 1511: Trinity, relationships between the persons (id: 1511)

The actual term “the Trinity” is not found in Scripture, but the truths implied in a trinitarian understanding of God are clearly set out. The OT hints at a plurality of persons in the Godhead. The NT affirms that the Son and the Holy Spirit are divine.

There is only one God
---------------------

**[DEU 6:4](https://ref.ly/Deut6:4)** *See also* [ISA 43:10–43:11](https://ref.ly/Isa43:10-Isa43:11), [ISA 44:8](https://ref.ly/Isa44:8); [1TI 1:17](https://ref.ly/1Tim1:17), [1TI 2:5](https://ref.ly/1Tim2:5); [JAS 2:19](https://ref.ly/Jas2:19)

OT indications of plurality in the Godhead
------------------------------------------

### God refers to himself in the plural

**[GEN 1:26](https://ref.ly/Gen1:26)** *See also* [GEN 3:22](https://ref.ly/Gen3:22), [GEN 11:7](https://ref.ly/Gen11:7); [ISA 6:8](https://ref.ly/Isa6:8)

### The angel of the LORD

**[GEN 16:11–16:13](https://ref.ly/Gen16:11-Gen16:13)** *The “angel of the LORD” is identified with, yet distinct from, God.* *See also* [GEN 18:1–18:33](https://ref.ly/Gen18:1-Gen18:33); [EXO 3:2–3:6](https://ref.ly/Exod3:2-Exod3:6); [JDG 13:3–13:22](https://ref.ly/Judg13:3-Judg13:22)

### The word of God

The `word of the LORD'' or`wisdom of God'' is personified and identified with, yet distinct from, God: [PSA 33:4](https://ref.ly/Ps33:4); ; [PRO 8:22–8:31](https://ref.ly/Prov8:22-Prov8:31)

### The Spirit of God

The Spirit of God is God's personal agent: [GEN 1:2](https://ref.ly/Gen1:2); ; [NEH 9:20](https://ref.ly/Neh9:20); ; [JOB 33:4](https://ref.ly/Job33:4); ; [ISA 40:13](https://ref.ly/Isa40:13)

### The Messiah

The Messiah's divine nature is emphasised: [PSA 110:1](https://ref.ly/Ps110:1); ; [ISA 9:6](https://ref.ly/Isa9:6); ; [JER 23:5–23:6](https://ref.ly/Jer23:5-Jer23:6)

### Interchangeable expressions

Word, Spirit (or breath) and LORD are used interchangeably for God: [PSA 33:6](https://ref.ly/Ps33:6); ; [ISA 48:16](https://ref.ly/Isa48:16), [ISA 61:1](https://ref.ly/Isa61:1)
