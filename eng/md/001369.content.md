## 5848: exaggeration (id: 5848)

An overstatement of something, usually relating to its importance or value. People exaggerate because of fear, envy, pride or self\-pity. Exaggeration can also be used positively to good effect, for example, in the teaching of Jesus Christ.

Exaggeration of difficulties through fear
-----------------------------------------

**[NUM 13:32–13:33](https://ref.ly/Num13:32-Num13:33)** *See also* [DEU 1:28](https://ref.ly/Deut1:28), [DEU 9:1](https://ref.ly/Deut9:1)

Exaggeration of the lifestyle of others through envy
----------------------------------------------------

**[JOB 21:7–21:10](https://ref.ly/Job21:7-Job21:10)** *Job's complaint that the wicked often appear to prosper is stated with exaggeration in order to make his point clear.* *See also* [PSA 73:3–73:12](https://ref.ly/Ps73:3-Ps73:12)

Exaggerating one's status or abilities through pride
----------------------------------------------------

**[EZK 27:3](https://ref.ly/Ezek27:3)** *See also* [EZK 28:2](https://ref.ly/Ezek28:2); [HOS 12:8](https://ref.ly/Hos12:8); [MAT 8:19](https://ref.ly/Matt8:19) parallel [LUK 9:57](https://ref.ly/Luke9:57); [LUK 22:33](https://ref.ly/Luke22:33); [ACT 12:21–12:23](https://ref.ly/Acts12:21-Acts12:23); [1CO 4:8](https://ref.ly/1Cor4:8); [REV 3:17](https://ref.ly/Rev3:17)

Exaggeration of the achievements of others
------------------------------------------

**[1SA 21:11](https://ref.ly/1Sam21:11)** *The reference to David as king by the Philistines may be an exaggeration reflecting the great success and popularity of David among the Israelite people.* *See also* [1SA 18:7](https://ref.ly/1Sam18:7), [1SA 29:5](https://ref.ly/1Sam29:5)
