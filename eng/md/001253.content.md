## 5712: marriage, between God and his people (id: 5712)

Marriage is used to describe the relationship between God and Israel in the OT and between Jesus Christ and the church in the NT. Contemplating marriage deepens understanding of God's love for his people; examining God's covenant love for his people similarly enriches an understanding of marriage.

God's marriage relationship with Israel
---------------------------------------

### God's marriage covenant with Israel

**[EZK 16:8–16:14](https://ref.ly/Ezek16:8-Ezek16:14)** *The covenant at Sinai was seen as a form of marriage.* *See also* [JER 31:32](https://ref.ly/Jer31:32); [EZK 16:59–16:60](https://ref.ly/Ezek16:59-Ezek16:60)

### God as Israel's husband

**[ISA 54:5](https://ref.ly/Isa54:5)** *See also* [HOS 2:7](https://ref.ly/Hos2:7); [JOL 1:8](https://ref.ly/Joel1:8)

### Israel's early devotion

**[JER 2:2](https://ref.ly/Jer2:2)** *See also* [EZK 16:43](https://ref.ly/Ezek16:43); [HOS 2:15](https://ref.ly/Hos2:15)

The breakdown of God's marriage to Israel
-----------------------------------------

### Israel's adultery

**[JER 3:20](https://ref.ly/Jer3:20)** *See also* [JER 2:32](https://ref.ly/Jer2:32); [EZK 16:32–16:34](https://ref.ly/Ezek16:32-Ezek16:34); [HOS 1:2](https://ref.ly/Hos1:2), [HOS 9:1](https://ref.ly/Hos9:1)

### Israel's alienation from God is likened to a divorce

**[HOS 2:2](https://ref.ly/Hos2:2)** *Israel's unfaithfulness led to a form of divorce between God and his people. This was sometimes identified with the exile.* *See also* [ISA 50:1](https://ref.ly/Isa50:1); [JER 3:6–3:10](https://ref.ly/Jer3:6-Jer3:10)
