## 6750: sin-bearer (id: 6750)

A person or animal that acts in God's sight in a substitutionary capacity, to whom are transferred the sins of others, together with the corresponding penalty for those sins.

The principle of accountability necessitates a sin\-bearer
----------------------------------------------------------

### All are held responsible for their own sin and its consequences

**[LEV 5:17](https://ref.ly/Lev5:17)** *See also* [LEV 5:1](https://ref.ly/Lev5:1), [LEV 20:16–20:21](https://ref.ly/Lev20:16-Lev20:21), [LEV 24:15–24:16](https://ref.ly/Lev24:15-Lev24:16); [EZK 18:19–18:20](https://ref.ly/Ezek18:19-Ezek18:20)

### Individuals and nations come under God's judgment for their sin

**[EZK 4:4–4:6](https://ref.ly/Ezek4:4-Ezek4:6)** *Ezekiel's behaviour foreshadowed God's judgment on the sin of Israel and Judah.* *See also* [EZK 16:58](https://ref.ly/Ezek16:58), [EZK 18:4](https://ref.ly/Ezek18:4); [MAT 11:21–11:24](https://ref.ly/Matt11:21-Matt11:24) parallel [LUK 10:12–10:15](https://ref.ly/Luke10:12-Luke10:15); [ROM 1:18–1:32](https://ref.ly/Rom1:18-Rom1:32), [ROM 6:23](https://ref.ly/Rom6:23)

God's gracious provision of a sin\-bearer in the OT
---------------------------------------------------

### God takes away the sin of those who repent

**[ISA 44:22](https://ref.ly/Isa44:22)** *See also* [2SA 12:13](https://ref.ly/2Sam12:13); [PSA 103:12](https://ref.ly/Ps103:12); [ZEC 3:4](https://ref.ly/Zech3:4)

### The sacrifice of an animal to remove sin

**[LEV 5:5–5:6](https://ref.ly/Lev5:5-Lev5:6)** *See also* [LEV 4:1–4:35](https://ref.ly/Lev4:1-Lev4:35), [LEV 5:14–6:7](https://ref.ly/Lev5:14-Lev6:7)

### The Day of Atonement and the removal of sin

**[LEV 16:8–16:10](https://ref.ly/Lev16:8-Lev16:10)** *The sacrificial goat expiated the sins of the people in the sight of God; the driving away of the scapegoat into the desert symbolised the removal of the sin of the community.* *See also* [LEV 16:15–16:19](https://ref.ly/Lev16:15-Lev16:19) *the sin offering*; [LEV 16:20–16:22](https://ref.ly/Lev16:20-Lev16:22) *the scapegoat*
