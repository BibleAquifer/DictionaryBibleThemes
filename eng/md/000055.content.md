## 1255: face of God (id: 1255)

A term used to refer to the character of God, especially his favour towards his people. For God to turn his face to his people is to offer them his grace and help; for God to turn his face against his people is to withhold his favour and blessing. To seek the face of God is to seek his favour.

The visibility of God's face
----------------------------

### His face cannot be seen

**[EXO 33:20–33:23](https://ref.ly/Exod33:20-Exod33:23)** *“The face of God” may mean here God's real and true self, the inference being that no\-one can look on God as he is in his total purity and majesty.*

### Apparent exceptions where God is seen face to face

**[GEN 32:30](https://ref.ly/Gen32:30)** *After Jacob's wrestling with a figure whose exact identity is not explicit in the preceding verses. Jacob did experience a real encounter with God but God was in a real and approachable form, not in his transcendent and visible glory.* *See also* [EXO 33:11](https://ref.ly/Exod33:11); [NUM 12:8](https://ref.ly/Num12:8); [DEU 5:4](https://ref.ly/Deut5:4), [DEU 34:10](https://ref.ly/Deut34:10); [EZK 20:35](https://ref.ly/Ezek20:35)

### God must reveal himself if people are to see his face

[JOB 34:29](https://ref.ly/Job34:29) *The Greek word for “revelation” literally means “removing a veil from one's face”.*

### Appearing before God in worship

**[EXO 23:15](https://ref.ly/Exod23:15)** *The phrase seems to recall the fact that in ancient religions worshippers came before carved faces of their idols. In Israel such physical representations of God were forbidden but the phrase was still used underlining the belief that Israel's God was present in his sanctuary.* *See also* [EXO 23:17](https://ref.ly/Exod23:17), [EXO 34:20](https://ref.ly/Exod34:20); [DEU 16:16](https://ref.ly/Deut16:16), [DEU 31:11](https://ref.ly/Deut31:11); [PSA 42:2](https://ref.ly/Ps42:2); [ISA 1:12](https://ref.ly/Isa1:12)
