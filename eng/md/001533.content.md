## 6166: flesh, sinful nature (id: 6166)

The physical aspect of human beings, which distinguishes them from God and is therefore frequently used in the NT as a symbol of human sinful nature in contrast with God's perfection. (The Greek word for “flesh” is sometimes translated by other words and phrases in the passages cited in this theme.).

Flesh as the bodily substance of human beings
---------------------------------------------

### As individuals or in relation to others

**[PSA 84:2](https://ref.ly/Ps84:2)** *See also* [GEN 2:23–2:24](https://ref.ly/Gen2:23-Gen2:24), [GEN 29:14](https://ref.ly/Gen29:14); [1CO 15:39](https://ref.ly/1Cor15:39)GAL [2:20](https://ref.ly/1Cor2:20); [PHP 1:22–1:24](https://ref.ly/Phil1:22-Phil1:24)

### As the means by which Jesus Christ identified with the human race to bring salvation

**[JHN 1:14](https://ref.ly/John1:14)** *See also* [EPH 2:15](https://ref.ly/Eph2:15); [HEB 10:20](https://ref.ly/Heb10:20); [1JN 4:2](https://ref.ly/1John4:2)

### As subject to mortality

**[ISA 40:6–40:7](https://ref.ly/Isa40:6-Isa40:7)** *See also* [PSA 78:39](https://ref.ly/Ps78:39); [ACT 2:31](https://ref.ly/Acts2:31); [1CO 15:50](https://ref.ly/1Cor15:50)

### As subject to weakness

**[2CH 32:8](https://ref.ly/2Chr32:8)** *See also* [PSA 73:26](https://ref.ly/Ps73:26); [MAT 26:41](https://ref.ly/Matt26:41) parallel [MRK 14:38](https://ref.ly/Mark14:38)

Flesh as contrasting human nature with God's perfection
-------------------------------------------------------

### The powerlessness of human beings contrasted with God's eternal power

**[ISA 31:3](https://ref.ly/Isa31:3)** *See also* [JHN 3:6](https://ref.ly/John3:6), [JHN 6:63](https://ref.ly/John6:63)

### Human or worldly standards contrasted with God's standards

**[JHN 8:15](https://ref.ly/John8:15)** *See also* [1CO 1:26](https://ref.ly/1Cor1:26); [2CO 5:16](https://ref.ly/2Cor5:16), [2CO 10:3–10:4](https://ref.ly/2Cor10:3-2Cor10:4)

Flesh as denoting the sinful nature of human beings
---------------------------------------------------

### The tendency to sin

**[ROM 7:18](https://ref.ly/Rom7:18)** *Paul does not mean that no goodness at all exists in people; nor that the physical aspect of human beings is inherently evil. He means that humans are invariably infected by evil and subject to its power.* *See also* [JER 17:5](https://ref.ly/Jer17:5)

### The conflict in human experience between the sinful nature and the Spirit of God

**[GAL 5:17](https://ref.ly/Gal5:17)** *See also* [ROM 8:4–8:9](https://ref.ly/Rom8:4-Rom8:9); [GAL 5:19–5:25](https://ref.ly/Gal5:19-Gal5:25)

The sinful nature is opposed to God and his will
------------------------------------------------

### This opposition finds expression in a range of acts and attitudes

**[GAL 5:19–5:21](https://ref.ly/Gal5:19-Gal5:21)** *See also* [ROM 7:14–7:25](https://ref.ly/Rom7:14-Rom7:25), [ROM 8:7](https://ref.ly/Rom8:7), [ROM 13:13–13:14](https://ref.ly/Rom13:13-Rom13:14); [1CO 6:9–6:11](https://ref.ly/1Cor6:9-1Cor6:11); [EPH 5:5](https://ref.ly/Eph5:5); [JAS 1:14–1:15](https://ref.ly/Jas1:14-Jas1:15); [1PE 2:11](https://ref.ly/1Pet2:11); [2PE 2:10](https://ref.ly/2Pet2:10), [2PE 2:18](https://ref.ly/2Pet2:18); [1JN 2:16](https://ref.ly/1John2:16)

### Confidence in the law is futile

**[ROM 8:3](https://ref.ly/Rom8:3)** *Because of the sinfulness of human nature, God's law is powerless to bring people into relationship with God*; **[GAL 3:3](https://ref.ly/Gal3:3)** *Even the attempt to find acceptance with God through keeping his law is an act of the sinful nature because it involves rejecting his offer of salvation through his grace.* *See also* [ROM 7:25](https://ref.ly/Rom7:25); [PHP 3:3–3:9](https://ref.ly/Phil3:3-Phil3:9)

### The sinful nature controls human behaviour in ways which run counter to God's purpose

**[ROM 8:8](https://ref.ly/Rom8:8)** *See also* [ROM 7:5](https://ref.ly/Rom7:5)

The sinful nature therefore makes people subject to God's judgment and to death
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**[ROM 8:13](https://ref.ly/Rom8:13)**; **[EPH 2:3](https://ref.ly/Eph2:3)** *See also* [GAL 6:8](https://ref.ly/Gal6:8)

Believers are not controlled by the sinful nature
-------------------------------------------------

### Through Jesus Christ's entering into human flesh, God delivers from the power and consequences of human sinfulness

**[ROM 8:3](https://ref.ly/Rom8:3)** *See also* [EPH 2:15](https://ref.ly/Eph2:15)

### Believers have crucified the sinful nature

**[ROM 7:5–7:6](https://ref.ly/Rom7:5-Rom7:6)** *See also* [ROM 8:8–8:9](https://ref.ly/Rom8:8-Rom8:9); [GAL 5:24](https://ref.ly/Gal5:24); [COL 2:11](https://ref.ly/Col2:11)

### The power of God's Spirit enables believers to continue to resist the sinful nature

**[ROM 8:13](https://ref.ly/Rom8:13)** *See also* [ROM 13:14](https://ref.ly/Rom13:14); [GAL 5:13](https://ref.ly/Gal5:13); [COL 3:5–3:6](https://ref.ly/Col3:5-Col3:6); [1PE 2:11](https://ref.ly/1Pet2:11)

### God's provision of church discipline in eliminating the sinful nature

**[1CO 5:5](https://ref.ly/1Cor5:5)** *The discipline of excluding a sinner from the church community is intended to bring him to repentance and so abandon his sinful course of action. Repentance provoked by physical suffering is possibly also in mind.*
