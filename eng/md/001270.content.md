## 5732: polygamy (id: 5732)

The practice of having more than one wife at one time. Although not explicitly forbidden in Scripture as a general practice, it is presented as problematic and monogamy is the clearly preferred option.

Monogamy is God's intention for marriage
----------------------------------------

**[GEN 2:24](https://ref.ly/Gen2:24)** *See also* [JER 2:2](https://ref.ly/Jer2:2); [HOS 2:19–2:20](https://ref.ly/Hos2:19-Hos2:20); [EPH 5:31](https://ref.ly/Eph5:31)

Early examples of polygamy
--------------------------

**[GEN 4:19](https://ref.ly/Gen4:19)** *See also* [GEN 26:34](https://ref.ly/Gen26:34), [GEN 28:9](https://ref.ly/Gen28:9), [GEN 29:28](https://ref.ly/Gen29:28)

Reasons for the practice of polygamy
------------------------------------

### Sealing foreign treaties

**[1KI 3:1](https://ref.ly/1Kgs3:1)** *See also* [1KI 16:31](https://ref.ly/1Kgs16:31)

### Producing offspring

**[GEN 16:1–16:3](https://ref.ly/Gen16:1-Gen16:3)**; **[2CH 13:21](https://ref.ly/2Chr13:21)** *A large family, and especially numerous progeny, was regarded as a sign of divine blessing.* *See also* [GEN 30:3–30:5](https://ref.ly/Gen30:3-Gen30:5), [GEN 30:9](https://ref.ly/Gen30:9); [JDG 8:30](https://ref.ly/Judg8:30); [2SA 3:1–3:5](https://ref.ly/2Sam3:1-2Sam3:5); [1CH 7:4](https://ref.ly/1Chr7:4); [2CH 11:23](https://ref.ly/2Chr11:23), [2CH 24:2–24:3](https://ref.ly/2Chr24:2-2Chr24:3)

Problems associated with polygamy
---------------------------------

### Being led away from God

**[DEU 17:17](https://ref.ly/Deut17:17)** *It was common for kings to cement foreign alliances by marriage. Israel's king is warned against the practice*; **[1KI 11:1–11:8](https://ref.ly/1Kgs11:1-1Kgs11:8)** *See also* [NEH 13:25–13:26](https://ref.ly/Neh13:25-Neh13:26); [MAL 2:11](https://ref.ly/Mal2:11)

### Conflicts within families

**[GEN 29:30](https://ref.ly/Gen29:30), [GEN 30:1](https://ref.ly/Gen30:1)**; **[DEU 21:15–21:17](https://ref.ly/Deut21:15-Deut21:17)** *See also* [GEN 16:4–16:6](https://ref.ly/Gen16:4-Gen16:6) *Sarah and Hagar*; [1SA 1:4–1:7](https://ref.ly/1Sam1:4-1Sam1:7) *Hannah and Peninnah*; [2CH 11:21–11:22](https://ref.ly/2Chr11:21-2Chr11:22); [1KI 1:11–1:13](https://ref.ly/1Kgs1:11-1Kgs1:13) *the conflict over the succession to David's throne*
