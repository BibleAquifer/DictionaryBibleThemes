## 1680: types (id: 1680)

An OT institution, person, place or event regarded as anticipating the person of Jesus Christ or some aspect of the Christian faith or life.

OT foreshadowings of Jesus Christ
---------------------------------

**[LUK 24:25–24:27](https://ref.ly/Luke24:25-Luke24:27)** *See also* [LUK 24:44](https://ref.ly/Luke24:44); [1PE 1:10–1:12](https://ref.ly/1Pet1:10-1Pet1:12)

OT institutions as types
------------------------

### The Sabbath: Jesus Christ's finished work

**[HEB 4:3–4:6](https://ref.ly/Heb4:3-Heb4:6)** *See also* [GEN 2:2–2:3](https://ref.ly/Gen2:2-Gen2:3); [HEB 4:9–4:10](https://ref.ly/Heb4:9-Heb4:10)

### Marriage: Jesus Christ's union with his people

**[EPH 5:30–5:32](https://ref.ly/Eph5:30-Eph5:32)** *See also* [GEN 2:22–2:24](https://ref.ly/Gen2:22-Gen2:24) *Adam may be viewed as a figure of Jesus Christ, and Eve, of the church, formed in Christ, loved and united with him*; [PSA 45:10–45:11](https://ref.ly/Ps45:10-Ps45:11); [MAT 9:15](https://ref.ly/Matt9:15), [MAT 25:1](https://ref.ly/Matt25:1); [JHN 3:29](https://ref.ly/John3:29); [EPH 5:25–5:27](https://ref.ly/Eph5:25-Eph5:27); [REV 19:7](https://ref.ly/Rev19:7), [REV 21:2](https://ref.ly/Rev21:2)

### Circumcision: believers' union with Christ

**[COL 2:11](https://ref.ly/Col2:11)** *See also* [GEN 17:9–17:14](https://ref.ly/Gen17:9-Gen17:14); [ROM 2:29](https://ref.ly/Rom2:29); [PHP 3:3](https://ref.ly/Phil3:3)

### The tabernacle: Jesus Christ's coming among his people

**[JHN 1:14](https://ref.ly/John1:14)** *The context shows that “The Word” is Jesus Christ; “made his dwelling” involves the idea of pitching a tent and reflects the tabernacle which was pitched among the Israelites and filled with God's glory; so Christ has pitched his tent among his people and revealed his glory.* *See also* [EXO 25:8](https://ref.ly/Exod25:8), [EXO 40:34–40:35](https://ref.ly/Exod40:34-Exod40:35)

### The tabernacle: the way to God

**[HEB 9:11–9:12](https://ref.ly/Heb9:11-Heb9:12)** *See also* [EXO 25:22](https://ref.ly/Exod25:22), [EXO 30:6](https://ref.ly/Exod30:6), [EXO 30:36](https://ref.ly/Exod30:36); [NUM 17:4](https://ref.ly/Num17:4); [HEB 9:8](https://ref.ly/Heb9:8), [HEB 10:19–10:22](https://ref.ly/Heb10:19-Heb10:22)

### The high priesthood: Jesus Christ as intercessor

**[HEB 7:23–7:28](https://ref.ly/Heb7:23-Heb7:28)** *See also* [EXO 29:1–29:7](https://ref.ly/Exod29:1-Exod29:7); [LEV 16:11–16:17](https://ref.ly/Lev16:11-Lev16:17); [HEB 4:14–4:16](https://ref.ly/Heb4:14-Heb4:16), [HEB 5:1–5:5](https://ref.ly/Heb5:1-Heb5:5) *Jesus Christ, the great high priest, is both human and divine*; [HEB 9:11–9:12](https://ref.ly/Heb9:11-Heb9:12), [HEB 10:11–10:12](https://ref.ly/Heb10:11-Heb10:12) *Note the contrast between the many OT sacrifices and Jesus Christ's once\-for\-all offering of himself.*

### The priesthood: Christian worship and service

**[HEB 13:15–13:16](https://ref.ly/Heb13:15-Heb13:16)** *See also* [EXO 19:6](https://ref.ly/Exod19:6), [EXO 29:8–29:9](https://ref.ly/Exod29:8-Exod29:9); [1PE 2:5](https://ref.ly/1Pet2:5); [REV 1:6](https://ref.ly/Rev1:6)

### Sacrifices: Jesus Christ as substitute

**[ROM 3:25](https://ref.ly/Rom3:25)** *See also* [LEV 4:13–4:15](https://ref.ly/Lev4:13-Lev4:15), [LEV 4:25–4:35](https://ref.ly/Lev4:25-Lev4:35) *Hands laid on the head of the sacrifice symbolised the transfer of guilt and its consequences to another. A perfect animal was required*; [ISA 53:4–53:12](https://ref.ly/Isa53:4-Isa53:12); [MRK 15:25–15:37](https://ref.ly/Mark15:25-Mark15:37); [2CO 5:15](https://ref.ly/2Cor5:15); [GAL 2:20](https://ref.ly/Gal2:20); [1PE 3:18](https://ref.ly/1Pet3:18)

### Sacrifice: believers' consecration

**[ROM 12:1](https://ref.ly/Rom12:1)** *See also* [LEV 1:1–1:9](https://ref.ly/Lev1:1-Lev1:9); [NUM 15:1–15:12](https://ref.ly/Num15:1-Num15:12); [PHP 2:17](https://ref.ly/Phil2:17); [2TI 4:6](https://ref.ly/2Tim4:6)

### The day of atonement: Jesus Christ as sin\-bearer

**[HEB 9:28](https://ref.ly/Heb9:28)** *See also* [LEV 16:1–16:34](https://ref.ly/Lev16:1-Lev16:34); [HEB 9:7](https://ref.ly/Heb9:7)

### Passover: deliverance from judgment

**[1CO 5:7](https://ref.ly/1Cor5:7)** *See also* [EXO 12:1–12:16](https://ref.ly/Exod12:1-Exod12:16); [MAT 26:17](https://ref.ly/Matt26:17) parallels [MRK 14:12](https://ref.ly/Mark14:12), [LUK 22:7](https://ref.ly/Luke22:7); [MAT 26:26–26:29](https://ref.ly/Matt26:26-Matt26:29) parallels [MRK 14:22–14:25](https://ref.ly/Mark14:22-Mark14:25), [LUK 22:14–22:20](https://ref.ly/Luke22:14-Luke22:20); [JHN 1:29](https://ref.ly/John1:29); [1PE 1:18–1:19](https://ref.ly/1Pet1:18-1Pet1:19)

### Redemption: release from sin

**[MAT 20:28](https://ref.ly/Matt20:28)** parallel [MRK 10:45](https://ref.ly/Mark10:45) *“Redemption” and “ransom” both involve buying back that which has been lost.* *See also* [EXO 13:11–13:13](https://ref.ly/Exod13:11-Exod13:13), [EXO 21:28–21:32](https://ref.ly/Exod21:28-Exod21:32); [JOB 19:25](https://ref.ly/Job19:25); [PSA 78:35](https://ref.ly/Ps78:35); [ISA 41:14](https://ref.ly/Isa41:14); [ROM 3:24](https://ref.ly/Rom3:24); [1CO 6:20](https://ref.ly/1Cor6:20)GAL [3:13](https://ref.ly/1Cor3:13), [GAL 4:5](https://ref.ly/Gal4:5)[EPH 1:7](https://ref.ly/Eph1:7) *Blood is the ransom price for the forgiveness of sin*; [1TI 2:6](https://ref.ly/1Tim2:6); [TIT 2:14](https://ref.ly/Titus2:14); [HEB 9:15](https://ref.ly/Heb9:15)

### The temple: the church

**[2CO 6:16](https://ref.ly/2Cor6:16)** *See also* [1KI 6:1](https://ref.ly/1Kgs6:1), [1KI 8:10–8:13](https://ref.ly/1Kgs8:10-1Kgs8:13); [1CO 3:16](https://ref.ly/1Cor3:16); [EPH 2:21–2:22](https://ref.ly/Eph2:21-Eph2:22); [1PE 2:5](https://ref.ly/1Pet2:5)
