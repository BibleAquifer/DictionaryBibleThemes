## 5228: battering-rams (id: 5228)

Battering\-rams used to make breaches in the walls of besieged cities
---------------------------------------------------------------------

### Historic references to the breaking down of walls

[2SA 20:15](https://ref.ly/2Sam20:15) *The wall of Abel Beth Maacah. References to breached or broken walls are evidence that the use of battering\-rams was common*; ; [2KI 14:13](https://ref.ly/2Kgs14:13) parallel [2CH 25:23](https://ref.ly/2Chr25:23); ; [JER 39:2](https://ref.ly/Jer39:2) parallels [2CH 36:19](https://ref.ly/2Chr36:19), [JER 52:7](https://ref.ly/Jer52:7); ; [NEH 1:3](https://ref.ly/Neh1:3)

### Figurative references to the breaking down of walls

[PSA 89:40](https://ref.ly/Ps89:40) *referring to God's displeasure with David's dynasty*

### Prophetic references to the breaking down of walls as a picture of the LORD's power

[ISA 22:5](https://ref.ly/Isa22:5), [ISA 24:12](https://ref.ly/Isa24:12); ; [EZK 21:22](https://ref.ly/Ezek21:22), [EZK 26:9](https://ref.ly/Ezek26:9); ; [MIC 2:13](https://ref.ly/Mic2:13)

### Peace is symbolised by no breaching of walls

[PSA 144:14](https://ref.ly/Ps144:14)

Model battering\-rams were part of the symbolic representation of the siege of Jerusalem
