## 5618: weights and measures, linear (id: 5618)

These terms were based on natural units of measurement that could be easily applied. Measures are given in ascending order, with their approximate imperial and metric equivalents.

Finger (1/4 handbreadth): about 3/4 inch (about 1\.85 centimetres)
------------------------------------------------------------------

**[JER 52:21](https://ref.ly/Jer52:21)** *The finger was 1/4 handbreadth and was the smallest subdivision of the cubit. This is its only mention in Scripture.*

Handbreadth (4 fingers): about 3 inches (about 8 centimetres)
-------------------------------------------------------------

**[EXO 25:25](https://ref.ly/Exod25:25)** parallel [EXO 37:12](https://ref.ly/Exod37:12) *The handbreadth was the width of the hand at the base of the four fingers.* *See also* [1KI 7:26](https://ref.ly/1Kgs7:26) parallel [2CH 4:5](https://ref.ly/2Chr4:5); [PSA 39:5](https://ref.ly/Ps39:5) *The handbreadth is here used figuratively of the shortness of human life*; [EZK 40:5](https://ref.ly/Ezek40:5)

Span (half a cubit): about 9 inches (about 23 centimetres)
----------------------------------------------------------

**[EXO 28:16](https://ref.ly/Exod28:16)** parallel [EXO 39:9](https://ref.ly/Exod39:9) *The span was the width of the outstretched hand from thumb to little finger.* *See also* [1SA 17:4](https://ref.ly/1Sam17:4) *Goliath's height is given in the Hebrew as “six cubits and a span”*; [PSA 90:10](https://ref.ly/Ps90:10) *The span is here used figuratively of the shortness of human life*; [EZK 43:13](https://ref.ly/Ezek43:13)

Cubit (2 spans): about 18 inches (about 0\.5 metre)
---------------------------------------------------

**[EXO 25:10](https://ref.ly/Exod25:10)** parallel [EXO 37:1](https://ref.ly/Exod37:1) *The cubit was the distance from the fingertip to the elbow, and was used to measure height, size, depth and distance.* *See also* [GEN 7:20](https://ref.ly/Gen7:20) *The Hebrew describes the depth of the water as “fifteen cubits”*; [EXO 27:9–27:18](https://ref.ly/Exod27:9-Exod27:18) parallel [EXO 38:9–38:15](https://ref.ly/Exod38:9-Exod38:15); [1KI 6:2–6:3](https://ref.ly/1Kgs6:2-1Kgs6:3) parallel [2CH 3:3–3:4](https://ref.ly/2Chr3:3-2Chr3:4); [1CH 11:23](https://ref.ly/1Chr11:23) *The Hebrew describes the man as “five cubits tall”*; [EZK 40:5](https://ref.ly/Ezek40:5), [EZK 45:1–45:6](https://ref.ly/Ezek45:1-Ezek45:6), [EZK 47:3–47:5](https://ref.ly/Ezek47:3-Ezek47:5); [JHN 21:8](https://ref.ly/John21:8) *The Greek text describes the distance as “about two hundred cubits”*; [REV 21:17](https://ref.ly/Rev21:17)

Reed (6 cubits): about 10 feet (about 3 metres)
-----------------------------------------------

**[EZK 41:8](https://ref.ly/Ezek41:8)** *Originally a measuring instrument, the reed became a recognised measure of six cubits (the rod).* *See also* [EZK 40:3](https://ref.ly/Ezek40:3), [EZK 40:5–40:7](https://ref.ly/Ezek40:5-Ezek40:7); [REV 11:1](https://ref.ly/Rev11:1), [REV 21:15–21:16](https://ref.ly/Rev21:15-Rev21:16)

Mile
----

**[MAT 5:41](https://ref.ly/Matt5:41)** *The Greek word for “mile” here is “milion”, a transliteration of the Roman measurement “mille passuum”, “a thousand paces”.* *See also* [LUK 24:13](https://ref.ly/Luke24:13) *The measurement in the Greek is “sixty stadia”. Eight stadia were about one mile (about 1480 metres)*; [JHN 6:19](https://ref.ly/John6:19) *The measurement in the Greek is “twenty\-five or thirty stadia”*; [JHN 11:18](https://ref.ly/John11:18) *The measurement in the Greek is “fifteen stadia”.*
