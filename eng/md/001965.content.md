## 7933: Lord's Supper (id: 7933)

The commemoration and remembrance of Jesus Christ's last supper, and all the benefits that result to believers. Other terms have been used subsequently by Christians, including “Communion” and “Eucharist”.

Terms for the Lord's Supper in the NT
-------------------------------------

[ACT 2:42](https://ref.ly/Acts2:42) *“breaking of bread” could mean both eating together and also celebrating the Lord's Supper*; ; [1CO 10:16](https://ref.ly/1Cor10:16) *The Greek word “koinonia”, here translated “participation”, is traditionally translated “communion”: hence the term “Holy Communion”*; ; [1CO 11:20](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:20) *the Lord's Supper*; ; [1CO 11:24](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:24) *Underlying “had given thanks” is the Greek “eucharisteo”, from which comes the term “Eucharist”.*

Jesus Christ's institution of the Lord's Supper
-----------------------------------------------

**[1CO 11:23–11:25](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:23-1Cor11:25)** parallels [MAT 26:26–26:28](https://ref.ly/Matt26:26-Matt26:28), [MRK 14:22–14:24](https://ref.ly/Mark14:22-Mark14:24), [LUK 22:17–22:20](https://ref.ly/Luke22:17-Luke22:20)

Celebrating the Lord's Supper in the NT
---------------------------------------

### As part of an ordinary meal

**[1CO 11:21](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:21)** *The church was not necessarily wrong to include the sacrament in an ordinary meal; this should have been conducted in a charitable way.*

### On the Lord's day

**[ACT 20:7](https://ref.ly/Acts20:7)** *See also* [JHN 20:26](https://ref.ly/John20:26)

### The fourfold formula for breaking bread: taking, giving thanks, breaking, giving

**[MAT 26:26](https://ref.ly/Matt26:26)** parallels [MRK 14:22](https://ref.ly/Mark14:22), [LUK 22:19](https://ref.ly/Luke22:19) *See also* [LUK 24:30](https://ref.ly/Luke24:30); [JHN 6:11](https://ref.ly/John6:11); [1CO 11:24](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:24)

### The sharing of the cup

**[1CO 11:25](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:25)** parallels [MAT 26:27–26:28](https://ref.ly/Matt26:27-Matt26:28), [MRK 14:23–14:24](https://ref.ly/Mark14:23-Mark14:24), [LUK 22:20](https://ref.ly/Luke22:20)

Themes connected with the Lord's Supper
---------------------------------------

### The Passover

**[1CO 5:7–5:8](https://ref.ly/1Cor5:7-1Cor5:8)** *See also* [JHN 11:50](https://ref.ly/John11:50) *Caiaphas' words may have an unintended Passover significance*; [JHN 13:1](https://ref.ly/John13:1), [JHN 19:14](https://ref.ly/John19:14), [JHN 19:33](https://ref.ly/John19:33), [JHN 19:36](https://ref.ly/John19:36); [EXO 12:46](https://ref.ly/Exod12:46); [NUM 9:12](https://ref.ly/Num9:12)

### The new covenant

**[1CO 11:25](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:25)** parallels [MAT 26:27–26:28](https://ref.ly/Matt26:27-Matt26:28), [MRK 14:23–14:24](https://ref.ly/Mark14:23-Mark14:24), [LUK 22:20](https://ref.ly/Luke22:20) *The reference to the (new) covenant implies a personal relationship with God and sins forgiven.*

### Remembrance

**[1CO 11:24](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:24)** parallel [LUK 22:19](https://ref.ly/Luke22:19)

### Thanksgiving, fellowship and unity

**[1CO 10:16](https://ref.ly/1Cor10:16)** *See also* [MAT 26:26–26:27](https://ref.ly/Matt26:26-Matt26:27) parallels [MRK 14:22–14:23](https://ref.ly/Mark14:22-Mark14:23), [LUK 22:19](https://ref.ly/Luke22:19); [1CO 11:20–11:21](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:20-1Cor11:21)

### The Lord's return

**[1CO 11:26](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:26)** *See also* [MAT 26:29](https://ref.ly/Matt26:29) parallels [MRK 14:25](https://ref.ly/Mark14:25), [LUK 22:16](https://ref.ly/Luke22:16); [1CO 16:22](https://ref.ly/1Cor16:22); [REV 22:20](https://ref.ly/Rev22:20)

### Separation from sin

**[1CO 10:21](https://ref.ly/1Cor10:21)** *Paul is referring to feasts in heathen temples.* *See also* [1CO 11:27–11:32](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:27-1Cor11:32)

### A foretaste of heaven

**[MAT 26:29](https://ref.ly/Matt26:29)** parallel [MRK 14:25](https://ref.ly/Mark14:25)
