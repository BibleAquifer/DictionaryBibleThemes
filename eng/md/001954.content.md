## 7908: baptism, significance of (id: 7908)

The NT uses a variety of images to explain the meaning of baptism, such as dying and rising with Christ, sharing in his death and being cleansed from sin.

Baptism is a symbol of the death of Jesus Christ
------------------------------------------------

**[LUK 12:50](https://ref.ly/Luke12:50)** *Jesus Christ described his death as a baptism or a flood overwhelming him.* *See also* [PSA 42:7](https://ref.ly/Ps42:7), [PSA 69:1–69:2](https://ref.ly/Ps69:1-Ps69:2), [PSA 88:7](https://ref.ly/Ps88:7); [MRK 10:38–10:39](https://ref.ly/Mark10:38-Mark10:39)

Baptism is a symbol of the burial of Jesus Christ
-------------------------------------------------

**[ROM 6:3–6:4](https://ref.ly/Rom6:3-Rom6:4)** *Immersion in water symbolises how the old sinful life is buried with Christ.* [ROM 8:13](https://ref.ly/Rom8:13); ; [COL 3:5](https://ref.ly/Col3:5)

Baptism is a symbol of being saved from the flood
-------------------------------------------------

**[1PE 3:21](https://ref.ly/1Pet3:21)** *The flood/baptism symbolises both judgment (the death of sinners and the death of Jesus Christ) and salvation (those in the ark and those in Christ passing safely through judgment).* *See also* [GEN 7:6–7:7](https://ref.ly/Gen7:6-Gen7:7)

Baptism is the gospel equivalent of circumcision
------------------------------------------------

**[COL 2:11–2:12](https://ref.ly/Col2:11-Col2:12)** *The “circumcision in Christ” may refer either to his death on the cross or to baptism.* *See also* [DEU 10:16](https://ref.ly/Deut10:16)

Baptism recalls the exodus
--------------------------

**[1CO 10:1–10:2](https://ref.ly/1Cor10:1-1Cor10:2)** *In and through the exodus experience Israel was united with and obedient to Moses, as Jesus Christ's people are to him.* *See also* [EXO 14:19–14:24](https://ref.ly/Exod14:19-Exod14:24)

Baptism symbolises washing from sin
-----------------------------------

**[ACT 22:16](https://ref.ly/Acts22:16)** *Baptism is an outward sign of cleansing from sin and from a defiled life and conscience.* *See also* [1CO 6:11](https://ref.ly/1Cor6:11); [TIT 3:5](https://ref.ly/Titus3:5); [1PE 3:21](https://ref.ly/1Pet3:21)

Baptism is a symbol of putting on Christ
----------------------------------------

**[GAL 3:27](https://ref.ly/Gal3:27)** *Baptism is seen as putting on Christ, as one might put on a coat or garment, and thus be “clothed with Christ”.*

Baptism as a symbol of unity
----------------------------

**[EPH 4:5](https://ref.ly/Eph4:5)** *See also* [1CO 12:13](https://ref.ly/1Cor12:13)
