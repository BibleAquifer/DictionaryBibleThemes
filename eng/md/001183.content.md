## 5621: wheel (id: 5621)

Early wheels were solid wooden discs. These were replaced around 1500 B.C. by lighter, spoked wheels. The most common use of wheels is on chariots and carts; they were also used by potters and in machinery for drawing water from a well. The chariot throne of God has wheels which are vividly described.

Chariot wheels
--------------

**[EXO 14:25](https://ref.ly/Exod14:25)**; **[NAM 3:2](https://ref.ly/Nah3:2)** *The sound of chariot wheels announces the approach of the enemy.* *See also* [2KI 7:6](https://ref.ly/2Kgs7:6); [ISA 5:28](https://ref.ly/Isa5:28); [JER 47:3](https://ref.ly/Jer47:3); [JOL 2:5](https://ref.ly/Joel2:5); [REV 9:9](https://ref.ly/Rev9:9)

Wagon wheels
------------

The Hebrew word for `wheel'' is here used for`wagon'': [EZK 23:24](https://ref.ly/Ezek23:24), [EZK 26:10](https://ref.ly/Ezek26:10)

Threshing wheels
----------------

**[PRO 20:26](https://ref.ly/Prov20:26)** *Threshing was carried out by driving a sledge or a cart with heavy wheels over the grain.* *See also* [ISA 28:27–28:28](https://ref.ly/Isa28:27-Isa28:28)

Other examples of wheels
------------------------

**[1KI 7:32–7:33](https://ref.ly/1Kgs7:32-1Kgs7:33)** *The stands for basins in Solomon's temple were mounted on wheels.* *See also* [1KI 7:30](https://ref.ly/1Kgs7:30); [ECC 12:6](https://ref.ly/Eccl12:6) *A wheel formed part of the machinery for drawing water from a well*; [JER 18:3](https://ref.ly/Jer18:3) *Potters turned their clay on a wheel.*
