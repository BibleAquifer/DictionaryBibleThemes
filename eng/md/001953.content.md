## 7907: baptism, practice of (id: 7907)

Baptism is associated with repenting of sin, believing the gospel message and becoming a member of Christ's body.

Baptism is ordained by Jesus Christ himself
-------------------------------------------

**[MAT 28:19](https://ref.ly/Matt28:19)** *See also* [ACT 9:17–9:18](https://ref.ly/Acts9:17-Acts9:18), [ACT 16:14–16:15](https://ref.ly/Acts16:14-Acts16:15)

Baptism is linked with repentance
---------------------------------

**[ACT 2:38](https://ref.ly/Acts2:38)** *See also* [HEB 6:1–6:2](https://ref.ly/Heb6:1-Heb6:2) *The “baptisms” referred to here may imply teaching about Jewish rituals or the baptism offered by John the Baptist.*

Baptism follows the decision to believe
---------------------------------------

**[ACT 2:41](https://ref.ly/Acts2:41), [ACT 18:8](https://ref.ly/Acts18:8)** *See also* [ACT 8:12–8:13](https://ref.ly/Acts8:12-Acts8:13), [ACT 16:31–16:33](https://ref.ly/Acts16:31-Acts16:33)

Baptism in the name of God or Jesus Christ
------------------------------------------

### Baptism in or into the name of Jesus Christ

**[ACT 19:5](https://ref.ly/Acts19:5)** *See also* [ACT 2:38](https://ref.ly/Acts2:38), [ACT 8:16](https://ref.ly/Acts8:16), [ACT 10:48](https://ref.ly/Acts10:48)

### Baptism in the name of the Trinity

[MAT 28:18–28:20](https://ref.ly/Matt28:18-Matt28:20)

Baptism was by immersion
------------------------

**[ROM 6:4](https://ref.ly/Rom6:4)** *The word “buried” implies immersion.* *See also* [ACT 8:38](https://ref.ly/Acts8:38)

Baptism is linked with the gift of the Holy Spirit
--------------------------------------------------

**[MRK 1:8](https://ref.ly/Mark1:8)**; **[ACT 1:5](https://ref.ly/Acts1:5)** *See also* [ACT 2:4](https://ref.ly/Acts2:4), [ACT 11:16](https://ref.ly/Acts11:16)**[1CO 12:13](https://ref.ly/1Cor12:13)** *All the Christians at Corinth had been spiritually baptised into Christ and thus united with him.*

Manifestations of the Holy Spirit may follow or precede water baptism
---------------------------------------------------------------------

*See also* [ACT 8:12–8:17](https://ref.ly/Acts8:12-Acts8:17), [ACT 9:17–9:18](https://ref.ly/Acts9:17-Acts9:18), [ACT 10:44–10:48](https://ref.ly/Acts10:44-Acts10:48)

The person who baptises is of little importance
-----------------------------------------------

**[1CO 1:14](https://ref.ly/1Cor1:14)** *What matters is not who baptises but the Saviour in whose name it is done.*

Baptism \`\`for the dead''
--------------------------

**[1CO 15:29](https://ref.ly/1Cor15:29)** *It is not certain if there was a ceremony of baptism “for the dead” in the NT church, nor whether, if it existed, Paul approved of it.*

Passages which may imply infant baptism
---------------------------------------

The \`\`households'' (not merely individuals) may well have included children: [ACT 16:15](https://ref.ly/Acts16:15), [ACT 16:33](https://ref.ly/Acts16:33), [ACT 18:8](https://ref.ly/Acts18:8); ; [1CO 1:16](https://ref.ly/1Cor1:16); [MRK 10:13–10:16](https://ref.ly/Mark10:13-Mark10:16); ; [1CO 7:14](https://ref.ly/1Cor7:14); ; [COL 2:11–2:12](https://ref.ly/Col2:11-Col2:12) *If baptism is here equated with circumcision, this suggests baptism is the sign of the new covenant.*

Passages apparently negating infant baptism
-------------------------------------------

[ACT 2:38–2:41](https://ref.ly/Acts2:38-Acts2:41) *infants cannot repent and believe*; ; [GAL 3:7](https://ref.ly/Gal3:7) *Under the new covenant it is faith in Jesus Christ, not an outward sign, that makes a person a child of God.*
