## 1225: God, as Spirit (id: 1225)

God has no material body and he is not subject to human limitations. While his spiritual nature is clearly inferred in Scripture, it is seldom directly stated; the emphasis is on his dynamic, living personhood.

God as spirit
-------------

**[JHN 4:24](https://ref.ly/John4:24)** *See also* [ISA 28:5–28:6](https://ref.ly/Isa28:5-Isa28:6), [ISA 31:3](https://ref.ly/Isa31:3)

### God is not subject to physical limitations

[1SA 16:7](https://ref.ly/1Sam16:7); ; [1KI 18:27](https://ref.ly/1Kgs18:27) *Elijah's mockery of Baal implies a contrast with the true God who is not subject to the limitations suggested*; ; [JOB 10:4–10:8](https://ref.ly/Job10:4-Job10:8); ; [PSA 121:3–121:4](https://ref.ly/Ps121:3-Ps121:4)

### God as the Spirit

**[PSA 139:7](https://ref.ly/Ps139:7)** *See also* [PSA 51:11](https://ref.ly/Ps51:11) *The Spirit and the presence of God are equated*; [PSA 143:10](https://ref.ly/Ps143:10) *God's guidance and the Spirit's leading are equated*; [ISA 34:16](https://ref.ly/Isa34:16) *God's Spirit participates in God's plans*; [2CO 3:17–3:18](https://ref.ly/2Cor3:17-2Cor3:18)
