## 5155: hair (id: 5155)

Various conditions, both physical and spiritual, are indicated in Scripture by human hair. Animal hair is used for a variety of purposes.

Physical qualities of human hair
--------------------------------

### A woman's hair is attractive

**[SNG 7:5](https://ref.ly/Song7:5)** *See also* [2KI 9:30](https://ref.ly/2Kgs9:30); [SNG 4:1](https://ref.ly/Song4:1); [1CO 11:15](https://ref.ly/1Cor11:15)

### A man's hair shows vigour

**[JDG 16:17–16:22](https://ref.ly/Judg16:17-Judg16:22)** *See also* [2SA 14:26](https://ref.ly/2Sam14:26); [SNG 5:11](https://ref.ly/Song5:11)

### Grey hair shows the glory of old age

**[PRO 16:31](https://ref.ly/Prov16:31)** *See also* [LEV 19:32](https://ref.ly/Lev19:32) *“aged” may be translated “grey\-haired”*; [PRO 20:29](https://ref.ly/Prov20:29)

### Greying hair as a sign of declining strength

[HOS 7:9](https://ref.ly/Hos7:9)

### White hair as a sign of age and wisdom

**[REV 1:14](https://ref.ly/Rev1:14)** *See also* [DAN 7:9](https://ref.ly/Dan7:9)

Nazirite vows forbade the cutting of hair
-----------------------------------------

### The reason for taking a Nazirite vow, separation to the Lord

[NUM 6:1–6:2](https://ref.ly/Num6:1-Num6:2) *While attention is often focused on Nazirite prohibitions, it is important to note that the primary reason was for separating oneself to God. Both men and women could make the vow*; ; [NUM 6:8](https://ref.ly/Num6:8)

### The requirements of the Nazirite vow

[NUM 6:2–6:4](https://ref.ly/Num6:2-Num6:4) *Nazirites must abstain from all produce of the vine*; ; [NUM 6:5](https://ref.ly/Num6:5) *Nazirites must not cut their hair*; ; [NUM 6:6–6:7](https://ref.ly/Num6:6-Num6:7) *Nazirites must not touch a dead body.*

### The rules for re\-establishing a broken Nazirite vow

[NUM 6:9–6:12](https://ref.ly/Num6:9-Num6:12)

### The regulations for completing the Nazirite vow

[NUM 6:13–6:17](https://ref.ly/Num6:13-Num6:17) *The Nazirite vow is completed, first, through prescribed sacrifices*; ; [NUM 6:18](https://ref.ly/Num6:18) *The Nazirite vow is completed, second, through cutting off one's hair and burning it on the altar*; ; [NUM 6:19–6:20](https://ref.ly/Num6:19-Num6:20) *The Nazirite vow is completed, third, through ritual acts performed by the priest.*

### Examples of people dedicated to being lifelong Nazirites

Samson was a lifelong Nazirite: [JDG 13:2–13:7](https://ref.ly/Judg13:2-Judg13:7), [JDG 16:1–16:22](https://ref.ly/Judg16:1-Judg16:22); [1SA 1:10–1:11](https://ref.ly/1Sam1:10-1Sam1:11) *Samuel was a lifelong Nazirite*; ; [LUK 1:13–1:15](https://ref.ly/Luke1:13-Luke1:15) *John the Baptist was possibly a Nazirite.*

### Examples of temporary Nazirite vows

[ACT 18:18](https://ref.ly/Acts18:18), [ACT 21:20–21:26](https://ref.ly/Acts21:20-Acts21:26)

### Nazirites to be seen as gifts from God to his people

[AMO 2:11–2:12](https://ref.ly/Amos2:11-Amos2:12)
