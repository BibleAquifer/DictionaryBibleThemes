## 4410: banquets (id: 4410)

Grand, celebratory meals consisting usually of rich food and flowing wine. Banquets usually accompanied weddings, religious festivals and other celebrations. Prophecies of the Messianic age refer to a banquet to which the righteous will be invited.

Characteristics of banquets
---------------------------

### They were usually held in a hall

**[SNG 2:4](https://ref.ly/Song2:4)** *See also* [EST 7:8](https://ref.ly/Esth7:8); [DAN 5:10](https://ref.ly/Dan5:10)

### Honoured guests were given special places

**[LUK 14:7–14:11](https://ref.ly/Luke14:7-Luke14:11)** *See also* [PRO 25:6–25:7](https://ref.ly/Prov25:6-Prov25:7); [MAT 23:6](https://ref.ly/Matt23:6) parallels [MRK 12:39](https://ref.ly/Mark12:39), [LUK 20:46](https://ref.ly/Luke20:46)

### Guests reclined on couches

[EST 1:6](https://ref.ly/Esth1:6); ; [MAT 26:7](https://ref.ly/Matt26:7) parallels [MRK 14:3](https://ref.ly/Mark14:3), [LUK 7:36](https://ref.ly/Luke7:36); ; [JHN 12:2](https://ref.ly/John12:2)

### Invitations were sent out

[LUK 14:17](https://ref.ly/Luke14:17) parallel [MAT 22:3](https://ref.ly/Matt22:3)

Occasions for holding banquets
------------------------------

### To celebrate weddings

**[MAT 22:2](https://ref.ly/Matt22:2)** *See also* [GEN 29:21–29:22](https://ref.ly/Gen29:21-Gen29:22) *a feast celebrates Jacob's wedding*; [JDG 14:10](https://ref.ly/Judg14:10) *Samson gives a wedding feast*; [EST 2:18](https://ref.ly/Esth2:18) *Xerxes celebrates Esther's choice as queen with a banquet*; [MAT 25:10](https://ref.ly/Matt25:10); [LUK 12:36](https://ref.ly/Luke12:36); [JHN 2:8–2:9](https://ref.ly/John2:8-John2:9); [REV 19:9](https://ref.ly/Rev19:9) *the marriage supper of the Lamb*

### To celebrate birthdays

[GEN 40:20](https://ref.ly/Gen40:20); ; [MRK 6:21](https://ref.ly/Mark6:21)

### To celebrate other events

[GEN 21:8](https://ref.ly/Gen21:8) *Abraham celebrates Isaac's weaning.*; [EST 8:17](https://ref.ly/Esth8:17), [EST 9:17–9:19](https://ref.ly/Esth9:17-Esth9:19); [LUK 15:23](https://ref.ly/Luke15:23) *The father celebrates the prodigal son's return.*
