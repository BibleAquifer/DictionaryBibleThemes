## 5402: market (id: 5402)

The market was a central feature of life in biblical times. Some of the biblical terms for redemption are drawn from the market\-place.

Ancient market\-places
----------------------

[1KI 20:34](https://ref.ly/1Kgs20:34) *Ahab's treaty with Ben\-Hadad suggests that by the 9th century B.C. there was a highly developed system of international trade.*; [ISA 23:3](https://ref.ly/Isa23:3); ; [EZK 27:24](https://ref.ly/Ezek27:24)

---------------------------------

### Methods of trading

Cash: [GEN 23:10–23:18](https://ref.ly/Gen23:10-Gen23:18); ; [JHN 2:14–2:16](https://ref.ly/John2:14-John2:16); [GEN 47:17](https://ref.ly/Gen47:17); ; [HOS 3:2](https://ref.ly/Hos3:2)

[RUT 4:7](https://ref.ly/Ruth4:7) *with a sandal*; ; [JER 32:11–32:12](https://ref.ly/Jer32:11-Jer32:12) *by signature*

Biblical terms for redemption are drawn from market language
------------------------------------------------------------

### In the gospel call

[PRO 8:3–8:5](https://ref.ly/Prov8:3-Prov8:5) *The city gates were often places for business deals*; ; [ISA 1:18](https://ref.ly/Isa1:18), [ISA 55:1](https://ref.ly/Isa55:1), [ISA 55:2](https://ref.ly/Isa55:2); ; [MAT 13:45–13:46](https://ref.ly/Matt13:45-Matt13:46)

### God's people have been bought

**[ACT 20:28](https://ref.ly/Acts20:28)** *See also* [EXO 15:16](https://ref.ly/Exod15:16); [PSA 74:2](https://ref.ly/Ps74:2); [1CO 6:20](https://ref.ly/1Cor6:20), [1CO 7:23](https://ref.ly/1Cor7:23); [2PE 2:1](https://ref.ly/2Pet2:1); [REV 5:9](https://ref.ly/Rev5:9), [REV 14:4](https://ref.ly/Rev14:4)

### God's people have been redeemed

**[GAL 4:4–4:5](https://ref.ly/Gal4:4-Gal4:5)** *The Greek word “exagorazo” means “to buy” and was used, for example, of the buying of slaves.* *See also* [GAL 3:13–3:14](https://ref.ly/Gal3:13-Gal3:14); [REV 14:3](https://ref.ly/Rev14:3) *The Greek “agorazo”, translated here as “redeemed”, may also be translated as “bought”. Elsewhere (e.g., [Tit 2:14](https://ref.ly/Titus2:14); [1Pe 1:18](https://ref.ly/1Pet1:18)), “lutro” meaning “to release” is used, conveying a similar idea.*

### Redeeming the time

The Greek `exagorazo'' here translated as`make the most of'': [EPH 5:16](https://ref.ly/Eph5:16); ; [COL 4:5](https://ref.ly/Col4:5)
