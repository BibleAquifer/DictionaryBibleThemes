## 5711: marriage, restrictions concerning (id: 5711)

The OT law forbade intermarriage with people who worshipped idols because it threatened the covenant relationship with God and his people. Marriage with close relations was also forbidden. Remarriage is permissible following the death of a spouse and, in certain circumstances, following divorce.

Intermarriage with foreigners
-----------------------------

### Examples of marriages to foreigners

[GEN 38:2](https://ref.ly/Gen38:2), [GEN 41:45](https://ref.ly/Gen41:45); ; [EXO 2:21](https://ref.ly/Exod2:21); ; [LEV 24:10](https://ref.ly/Lev24:10); ; [RUT 1:4](https://ref.ly/Ruth1:4); ; [1CH 2:34–2:35](https://ref.ly/1Chr2:34-1Chr2:35); ; [EZR 10:18–10:44](https://ref.ly/Ezra10:18-Ezra10:44)

### Warnings against marrying foreigners

**[EXO 34:16](https://ref.ly/Exod34:16)** *See also* [DEU 7:3–7:4](https://ref.ly/Deut7:3-Deut7:4); [JOS 23:12–23:13](https://ref.ly/Josh23:12-Josh23:13)

### Intermarriage with foreigners led to idolatry

**[JDG 3:5–3:6](https://ref.ly/Judg3:5-Judg3:6)**; **[1KI 11:1–11:8](https://ref.ly/1Kgs11:1-1Kgs11:8)** *Royal marriages to foreigners brought disastrous consequences.* *See also* [1KI 16:31](https://ref.ly/1Kgs16:31); [MAL 2:11](https://ref.ly/Mal2:11)EZR [9:1–9:2](https://ref.ly/Mal9:1-Mal9:2), [EZR 9:14](https://ref.ly/Ezra9:14), [EZR 10:1–10:2](https://ref.ly/Ezra10:1-Ezra10:2); [NEH 13:23–13:27](https://ref.ly/Neh13:23-Neh13:27)

### Parents sought to avoid their children marrying foreigners

**[NEH 10:30](https://ref.ly/Neh10:30)** *See also* [GEN 24:3–24:4](https://ref.ly/Gen24:3-Gen24:4) *Abraham seeks a bride for Isaac from among his own people.*[GEN 26:34–26:35](https://ref.ly/Gen26:34-Gen26:35), [GEN 27:46](https://ref.ly/Gen27:46)[GEN 28:1–28:2](https://ref.ly/Gen28:1-Gen28:2) *Isaac forbids Jacob to marry a Canaanite woman*; [JDG 14:3](https://ref.ly/Judg14:3) *Samson's parents encourage him to marry an Israelite.*

### Marriage to foreigners may be permissible

[RUT 4:13](https://ref.ly/Ruth4:13) *Boaz' marriage to Ruth the Moabitess was acceptable because Ruth embraced the Israelite faith. Restrictions were on the grounds of religion not of race.*

### Foreign wives were put aside after the exile

Levirate marriage
-----------------

### The levirate law was instituted to preserve the dead father's name

**[DEU 25:5–25:10](https://ref.ly/Deut25:5-Deut25:10)**; **[RUT 4:10](https://ref.ly/Ruth4:10)** *Levirate marriage refers to the legal obligation of a brother\-in\-law to produce heirs for his dead brother by marrying his widow. When there was no brother\-in\-law responsibility fell to a near relative, also described as a “kinsman\-redeemer”.* *See also* [GEN 38:8](https://ref.ly/Gen38:8), [GEN 38:11](https://ref.ly/Gen38:11); [RUT 1:11–1:13](https://ref.ly/Ruth1:11-Ruth1:13), [RUT 3:9](https://ref.ly/Ruth3:9), [RUT 4:5](https://ref.ly/Ruth4:5); [MAT 22:24–22:26](https://ref.ly/Matt22:24-Matt22:26) parallels [MRK 12:19–12:22](https://ref.ly/Mark12:19-Mark12:22), [LUK 20:28–20:31](https://ref.ly/Luke20:28-Luke20:31)
