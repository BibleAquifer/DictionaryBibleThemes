## 6678: justification, and Jesus Christ's work (id: 6678)

On account of the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ, the demands of the law of God are met, and believers are granted the status of being righteous in the sight of God.

Justification is grounded in the death of Jesus Christ
------------------------------------------------------

### Jesus Christ's death shields believers from God's wrath

**[ROM 8:3–8:4](https://ref.ly/Rom8:3-Rom8:4)** *See also* [ROM 3:25–3:26](https://ref.ly/Rom3:25-Rom3:26); [GAL 3:13](https://ref.ly/Gal3:13); [1JN 2:2](https://ref.ly/1John2:2)

Justification means believers are reckoned as righteous through the death of Jesus Christ
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**[ROM 5:19](https://ref.ly/Rom5:19)**; **[1CO 1:30](https://ref.ly/1Cor1:30)**; **[2CO 5:21](https://ref.ly/2Cor5:21)** *See also* [1CO 6:9–6:11](https://ref.ly/1Cor6:9-1Cor6:11); [PHP 3:8–3:9](https://ref.ly/Phil3:8-Phil3:9) *The term “imputation” is used to refer to the process by which God treats believers as being righteous in his sight on account of Jesus Christ's death.*
