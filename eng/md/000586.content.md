## 4906: abolition (id: 4906)

The final ending and elimination of practices or states of affairs. Scripture looks forward to God's final abolition of the present order, through the inauguration of the new Jerusalem.

Things to be abolished in the kingdom of God
--------------------------------------------

### War and destruction

**[HOS 2:18](https://ref.ly/Hos2:18)** *See also* [PSA 46:9–46:10](https://ref.ly/Ps46:9-Ps46:10); [ISA 9:5](https://ref.ly/Isa9:5); [ZEC 9:10](https://ref.ly/Zech9:10)

### Illness and suffering

**[EXO 23:25–23:26](https://ref.ly/Exod23:25-Exod23:26)** *See also* [EXO 15:26](https://ref.ly/Exod15:26); [DEU 7:15](https://ref.ly/Deut7:15); [ISA 53:4](https://ref.ly/Isa53:4); [MAT 8:16–8:17](https://ref.ly/Matt8:16-Matt8:17) parallels [MRK 1:32–1:34](https://ref.ly/Mark1:32-Mark1:34), [LUK 4:40–4:41](https://ref.ly/Luke4:40-Luke4:41)

### Sin

**[ROM 6:6](https://ref.ly/Rom6:6)** *See also* [EZK 36:25–36:26](https://ref.ly/Ezek36:25-Ezek36:26) *God speaks here of his abolition of sinful, human hard\-heartedness*; [ROM 8:3–8:4](https://ref.ly/Rom8:3-Rom8:4); [HEB 9:26](https://ref.ly/Heb9:26)

The abolition of the temple
---------------------------

### Sacrifice in the temple abolished

**[DAN 11:31](https://ref.ly/Dan11:31)** *Antiochus Epiphanes abolished the daily sacrifice in the temple in 168 B.C., thereby attacking the symbolic centre of Israel's life.* *See also* [DAN 12:11](https://ref.ly/Dan12:11)

### Jesus Christ was accused of seeking to abolish the temple

**[MAT 26:61](https://ref.ly/Matt26:61)** parallel [MRK 14:58](https://ref.ly/Mark14:58) *See also* [MAT 21:12–21:13](https://ref.ly/Matt21:12-Matt21:13) parallels [MRK 11:15–11:17](https://ref.ly/Mark11:15-Mark11:17), [LUK 19:45–19:46](https://ref.ly/Luke19:45-Luke19:46) *Jesus Christ's action may have been understood as a symbolic abolition of a defiled sacrificial system*; [MAT 27:39–27:40](https://ref.ly/Matt27:39-Matt27:40) parallel [MRK 15:29–15:30](https://ref.ly/Mark15:29-Mark15:30); [ACT 6:14](https://ref.ly/Acts6:14)

### The final abolition of the temple in the new Jerusalem

**[REV 21:22](https://ref.ly/Rev21:22)**

Abolition and the law
---------------------

### The law has not been abolished

**[MAT 5:17–5:19](https://ref.ly/Matt5:17-Matt5:19)** *See also* [ROM 3:31](https://ref.ly/Rom3:31)

### The law has been abolished as an instrument of condemnation

**[COL 2:13–2:14](https://ref.ly/Col2:13-Col2:14)** *See also* [2CO 3:6–3:17](https://ref.ly/2Cor3:6-2Cor3:17); [EPH 2:14–2:16](https://ref.ly/Eph2:14-Eph2:16); [HEB 10:1–10:10](https://ref.ly/Heb10:1-Heb10:10)

### The law does not abolish God's promises

**[GAL 3:17–3:18](https://ref.ly/Gal3:17-Gal3:18)** *God did not abolish his promise of righteousness to Abraham when he gave the law, because the promise was not conditional upon keeping the law but is received by faith.* *See also* [ROM 4:14](https://ref.ly/Rom4:14)

### The offence of the cross is abolished by accepting circumcision

**[GAL 5:11](https://ref.ly/Gal5:11)** *Jews found offensive the Christians' claim that salvation did not come through keeping the law, but instead through faith in a crucified Messiah, whom the law had cursed. Paul regarded “the offence of the cross” as central to the gospel, and refused to make salvation conditional upon circumcision and obedience to the law.* *See also* [1CO 1:22–1:24](https://ref.ly/1Cor1:22-1Cor1:24), [1CO 2:2](https://ref.ly/1Cor2:2); [GAL 3:13](https://ref.ly/Gal3:13), [GAL 5:2](https://ref.ly/Gal5:2), [GAL 6:14–6:15](https://ref.ly/Gal6:14-Gal6:15)
