## 5672: concubines (id: 5672)

A woman, often a servant or slave, with whom a man had regular sexual relations, but to whom he was not married. A concubine did not have the rights of a wife and her children were not rightful heirs, though a wife might offer a servant to her husband as a concubine to have children on her behalf.

Concubines and wives
--------------------

### Concubines were distinguished from wives

**[1KI 11:3](https://ref.ly/1Kgs11:3)** *Having concubines alongside wives was accepted in a polygamous society.* *See also* [JDG 8:30–8:31](https://ref.ly/Judg8:30-Judg8:31); [2SA 5:13](https://ref.ly/2Sam5:13), [2SA 19:5](https://ref.ly/2Sam19:5); [2CH 11:21](https://ref.ly/2Chr11:21); [DAN 5:2–5:3](https://ref.ly/Dan5:2-Dan5:3)

### Concubines were sometimes called wives

**[GEN 16:3](https://ref.ly/Gen16:3)** *Hagar was Abraham's “wife” only in a secondary sense.* *See also* [GEN 25:1](https://ref.ly/Gen25:1) *Keturah was one of Abraham's concubines*; [JDG 19:1–19:4](https://ref.ly/Judg19:1-Judg19:4)

### Concubines did not have the rights of a wife

[GEN 21:8–21:14](https://ref.ly/Gen21:8-Gen21:14) *They were more easily dismissed*; ; [EXO 21:7–21:11](https://ref.ly/Exod21:7-Exod21:11) *They did have some legal protection.*

### Maidservants were given as concubines

**[GEN 30:3–30:6](https://ref.ly/Gen30:3-Gen30:6)** *Bilhah's sons would be regarded by Rachel as her own and would thus share the inheritance with Jacob's other sons.* *See also* [GEN 30:9](https://ref.ly/Gen30:9), [GEN 16:1–16:2](https://ref.ly/Gen16:1-Gen16:2); [GAL 4:22–4:23](https://ref.ly/Gal4:22-Gal4:23)

### The tension between concubines and wives

**[GEN 16:4–16:6](https://ref.ly/Gen16:4-Gen16:6)** *Though Abraham's concubine, Hagar's status was still that of servant.* *See also* [GEN 21:9](https://ref.ly/Gen21:9); [PRO 30:23](https://ref.ly/Prov30:23); [GAL 4:29](https://ref.ly/Gal4:29)

### Children born to concubines were not rightful heirs

**[GEN 21:10–21:12](https://ref.ly/Gen21:10-Gen21:12)** *See also* [GEN 25:5–25:6](https://ref.ly/Gen25:5-Gen25:6); [JDG 9:18](https://ref.ly/Judg9:18); [ROM 9:7](https://ref.ly/Rom9:7); [GAL 4:30](https://ref.ly/Gal4:30); [HEB 11:18](https://ref.ly/Heb11:18)

Concubines were a symbol of possession
--------------------------------------

### By taking Jacob's concubine, Reuben laid premature claim to his father's estate

**[GEN 35:22](https://ref.ly/Gen35:22)** *See also* [GEN 49:3–49:4](https://ref.ly/Gen49:3-Gen49:4); [1CH 5:1](https://ref.ly/1Chr5:1)

### Taking the concubine(s) of a king was tantamount to laying claim to the kingdom

**[2SA 3:7](https://ref.ly/2Sam3:7)** *Abner is accused here of trying to usurp Ish\-Bosheth's inheritance.* *See also* [2SA 12:8](https://ref.ly/2Sam12:8), [2SA 16:21–16:22](https://ref.ly/2Sam16:21-2Sam16:22); [1KI 2:21–2:22](https://ref.ly/1Kgs2:21-1Kgs2:22)
