## 6175: guilt, removal of (id: 6175)

Through the death of Jesus Christ, God has provided a means of removing guilt, thus opening the way for believers to know the blessings of a right relationship with him.

The removal of guilt is God's work
----------------------------------

**[JER 2:22](https://ref.ly/Jer2:22), [JER 50:20](https://ref.ly/Jer50:20)**; **[ROM 8:33](https://ref.ly/Rom8:33)** *“Justify” is a legal term which includes the meaning “to declare not guilty”.* *See also* [2SA 24:10](https://ref.ly/2Sam24:10) parallel [1CH 21:8](https://ref.ly/1Chr21:8); [PSA 32:1–32:5](https://ref.ly/Ps32:1-Ps32:5); [ISA 6:1–6:6](https://ref.ly/Isa6:1-Isa6:6)

The removal of guilt by Jesus Christ's sacrificial death
--------------------------------------------------------

**[ISA 53:10–53:12](https://ref.ly/Isa53:10-Isa53:12)** *This prophecy finds its ultimate fulfilment in the person of Jesus Christ*; **[HEB 9:14](https://ref.ly/Heb9:14)** *See also* [JHN 1:29](https://ref.ly/John1:29) *The point of John's image is that Jesus Christ will be sacrificed, as lambs were, to remove people's guilt*; [ROM 5:9](https://ref.ly/Rom5:9) *The blood of Jesus Christ referred to here is his shed blood, and therefore speaks of his (sacrificial) death*; [2CO 5:21](https://ref.ly/2Cor5:21) *Human guilt is transferred to Jesus Christ, in order that believers might become righteous before God*; [TIT 2:14](https://ref.ly/Titus2:14); [HEB 9:28](https://ref.ly/Heb9:28), [HEB 10:11–10:14](https://ref.ly/Heb10:11-Heb10:14); [1JN 1:7](https://ref.ly/1John1:7)
